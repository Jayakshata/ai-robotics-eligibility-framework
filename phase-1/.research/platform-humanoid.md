## 1. Definition & Scope
A **bipedal, roughly human-form, floating-base robot** aimed at **general-purpose physical work in human environments** — the bet that a human body plan plus a learned generalist policy can pick up the long tail of tasks built for people, on infrastructure (stairs, benches, tote walls, tools, door handles) designed for human reach and dexterity. What makes it distinct as an engineering target — harder than any sibling platform — is that it must solve **dynamic bipedal balance, whole-body coordination, and dexterous manipulation simultaneously**, on-board and in real time, with a **higher centre of mass and a smaller support polygon than a quadruped** (two feet, not four) *and* with the hands committed to work rather than to balance. The quadruped can plant four feet and use a back-mounted arm; the biped is underactuated at the ankles and must stay upright *while* both arms manipulate. It is the platform where the **teleoperation-vs-autonomy gap is widest**: as of mid-2026 leading platforms are, in the words of one industry survey, "impressively capable in demos and narrow tasks, but far from general-purpose autonomy," and "mainstream commercial adoption remains nascent" `✓ third-party`. The honest three-way split this report keeps throughout: **buy-today platforms** (research/education/dev units — Unitree, Fourier), **paid commercial pilots** (single-site, single-task RaaS — Agility at GXO, Figure at BMW, Apptronik at Mercedes, Atlas at Hyundai), and **demos / internal R&D** (Tesla Optimus, Sanctuary). Very little sits in the honest middle: a general-purpose humanoid autonomously earning its keep across varied tasks does not yet exist `✓ third-party`.

## 2. Signature Capability Stack
- **A4 Whole-Body & Balance Control** *(load-bearing — the platform's defining problem)* — coordinating 25–55 DoF under balance and contact constraints; keeping a high-CoM floating base upright while arms exert forces. ZMP/centroidal/MPC baselines increasingly wrapped or replaced by learned whole-body policies. → `whole-body-compliant-control`
- **A5 Bipedal Locomotion + Loco-Manipulation + Dexterous Manipulation** — dynamic walking/turning/stairs, manipulating *while* stepping (pulling a cart, carrying a tote), and multi-finger in-hand dexterity — three problems the humanoid must fuse. → `locomotion`, `manipulation-and-grasping`
- **A7 Robot Foundation Models / VLAs** *(the "brain," the frontier)* — generalist vision-language-action policies (GR00T, Figure Helix, Physical Intelligence π, Skild Brain) mapping pixels+language to whole-body action. The bet, and the least-proven layer. → `robot-foundation-models-vla`
- **A6 Imitation & RL** — sim-trained locomotion/whole-body policies (PPO in massively-parallel sim); teleoperation-demo imitation for manipulation; retargeting of **human motion-capture / video** onto the humanoid morphology. → `reinforcement-learning-control`
- **A1 Egocentric 3D Perception** — head/torso-camera 3D perception for navigation + manipulation in cluttered human spaces; tactile/palm-camera sensing at the hands. → `computer-vision-3d-perception`
- **A8 Sim-to-Real & Simulation** *(the enabling loop)* — domain randomization + actuator identification for **legs and hands together** (harder than legs alone); Isaac Lab / MuJoCo Playground pipelines. → `sim-to-real-transfer`, `simulation-digital-twins`
- **A9 HRI** and **A10 Safety** — operating a heavy, dynamic, fall-capable robot **in the same unfenced space as people** — the standards gap (see §3, §7).

## 3. Integration & Platform-Specific Skills
The "integration tax" no single capability owns: **dynamic bipedal balance** of an underactuated high-CoM floating base (capture-point / centroidal-momentum / MPC + whole-body QP, now commonly a learned WBC policy); **whole-body loco-manipulation** (the arms-and-legs-at-once coordination that Helix and GR00T target); **high-DoF real-time control on-board** within a tight power/thermal/compute budget; **sim-to-real for legged locomotion *and* dexterous manipulation jointly** (Isaac Lab / MuJoCo Playground), which is strictly harder than the quadruped's legs-only transfer; the **safety envelope for a fall-capable heavy robot near unprotected humans** — the hardest non-technical bar, because the industrial-arm standards (**ISO 10218**) and the cobot contact-limit spec (**ISO/TS 15066**, now folded into ISO 10218-2:2025) were written for **bounded, often fixed or slow-moving arms**, not a free-walking biped that can topple; **ISO 13482** (personal-care/service robots) is the nearest fit but is not a certification path most factory humanoids claim `✓`. Plus **dexterous-hand + tactile integration**, **battery/hot-swap and thermal budgeting** (runtime is a first-order constraint — see §6), and the **teleoperation-and-data pipeline** that is currently doing much of the real work behind "autonomous" demos.

## 4. Typical Verticals
**B1 manufacturing** — the beachhead, all **paid pilots inside fenced or designated zones**, not open-floor autonomy: **Figure 02→03 at BMW Plant Spartanburg** (Figure 02 ran an ~11-month trial "supporting production of 30,000+ X3 vehicles" inserting sheet-metal parts; Figure 03 now moving to a logistics **sequencing** task) `✓ BMW`; **Apptronik Apollo at Mercedes-Benz** (multi-plant, multi-task programme, plus GXO and contract-manufacturer Jabil — "designated areas") `~ vendor/press`; **Boston Dynamics electric Atlas at Hyundai** (2026 units committed to Hyundai + Google DeepMind, field-testing sequencing; broad plant deployment targeted **2028**) `⏱`; **Tesla Optimus** in Tesla's *own* factories only (internal "learning phase," no external customers) `✓`. **B2 logistics/warehouse** — **Agility Digit at GXO** under the industry's first humanoid **RaaS** contract (tote moving from cobots to conveyors, orchestrated by Agility Arc) — the most-cited commercial humanoid, but concentrated at **essentially one site** (near Atlanta, GA) `✓ GXO`. **B9 domestic/service** — **1X NEO** shipping to U.S. homes in 2026, but **teleoperation-assisted, not autonomous** (see §7); the longest-horizon, least-proven vertical. Chosen because all three are built for human bodies — human reach, human tools, human-sized totes and stairs — which is the entire thesis for the form factor.

## 5. Eligibility Profile
The archetypal **"humanoid robotics engineer"** bundles **whole-body / balance control + bipedal locomotion + dexterous manipulation**, increasingly + **VLA / robot-learning**. Strongest backgrounds: **Robotics / ME / EE** for the embodiment side (floating-base dynamics, centroidal/MPC + whole-body QP, high-torque actuation, contact mechanics, dexterous-hand mechatronics); **CS / AI** for the "brain" side (RL, imitation, VLAs, egocentric perception). Platform-specific must-haves: **RL whole-body / locomotion training in Isaac Lab and/or MuJoCo Playground**, **sim-to-real for legs+hands** (domain randomization, actuator nets), **whole-body MPC/QP**, **motion retargeting** from human mocap/video, **teleop-data** pipelines, and **ROS 2**. Frontier "brain" roles want `robot-foundation-models-vla` depth. Feeder capability profiles: `whole-body-compliant-control`, `locomotion`, `reinforcement-learning-control`, `manipulation-and-grasping`, `robot-foundation-models-vla` (with `sim-to-real-transfer` and `computer-vision-3d-perception` as strong support). Tiers: **L1** fleet/teleop operator + deployment tech → **L2–L3** whole-body-control / locomotion / manipulation software → **L4/L5** learned whole-body sim-to-real and humanoid-VLA research. The platform premium: **a real biped walking *and* manipulating robustly on hardware** — sim demos and teleoperated highlight reels are cheap; **whole-body sim-to-real on a real, fall-capable humanoid** is the differentiator.

## 6. Platform-Specific Hardware & Stack `~`
**Robots (status carefully distinguished; all specs/counts vendor-reported unless noted):**
- **Unitree G1 / H1** — **buy-today** volume leaders. G1 ~1.27–1.32 m, base list ~**$13.5–16k**, EDU configs ~$44–74k; **H1** full-size (~1.8 m, ~47 kg), list ~**$90k**, vendor "world's fastest humanoid" claim. Unitree shipped **~5,500 humanoids of ~14,600 sold globally in 2025 (~38%)** `~ third-party estimate` — but these are overwhelmingly **research/education/dev** units, not autonomous workers. `⏱`
- **Fourier GR-1 / GR-2 / GR-3** — Chinese; GR-1 (~1.65 m, 40 DoF, marketed "first mass-produced humanoid," 2023), GR-2 (~1.75 m, 53 DoF, 12-DoF tactile hands, swappable ~2 h battery), **GR-3 "Care-Bot"** (CES 2026, ~55 DoF, ~$27.5k+). Sold to research/industry (ETH, CMU cited) and used as **the primary real-robot test platform for NVIDIA GR00T**. `⏱`
- **Agility Digit** — **paid pilot / RaaS** (GXO); purpose-built legged warehouse robot (bird-legged, not anthropomorphic hands). Most-deployed *commercial* humanoid, ~single site. `✓ GXO`
- **Figure 02 / 03** — **paid pilot** (BMW). F.03 adds soft-cover safety, wireless charging, speech, and improved tactile/palm-camera hands; runs Figure's **Helix / Helix 02** VLA. `✓ BMW`
- **Boston Dynamics electric Atlas** — **paid pilot** (Hyundai + Google DeepMind). Production version unveiled **CES Jan 2026**; **Hyundai Mobis actuators**; integrates **NVIDIA Jetson Thor** on-board. `⏱`
- **Tesla Optimus** — **internal R&D / demo**. Gen 3 (first "mass-production" design) reveal **repeatedly delayed** through 2026; ~**300 units** in an internal "learning phase," **zero commercial sales**. `✓ third-party`
- **Apptronik Apollo** — **paid pilot** (Mercedes/GXO/Jabil); Google DeepMind AI partnership. `⏱`
- **1X NEO** — **shipping to homes 2026 but teleop-assisted**; 22-DoF hands, soft body, ~$20k or ~$499/mo. `⏱`
- **Sanctuary Phoenix** — **prototype/demo**; company **pivoted** to hardware-agnostic "Physical AI" software running on third-party industrial arms (see §7). `~`

**Actuators:** three families in tension — **harmonic (strain-wave) drives** (compact, high-reduction, precise, but stiff / poorly backdrivable, need lubrication, flexspline fatigue); **Quasi-Direct-Drive (QDD)** (low-ratio ~5:1–10:1 planetary + high-torque BLDC + FOC torque control — backdrivable, proprioceptive, compliant, now the trend for legs); **Series-Elastic (SEA)** (spring in series for force control / shock tolerance). Hands and small wrists still lean on harmonic/high-reduction for density (e.g. MinebeaMitsumi + Harmonic Drive Systems micro-actuators shown at CES 2026). `✓`
**Dexterous hands:** the visible frontier and a real bottleneck — 11–22 DoF hands with tactile arrays and palm cameras (1X NEO 22-DoF, F.03 tactile+palm-cam, RealHand piano demo at CES 2026); still far short of human dexterity in the field. `✓`
**Battery / runtime:** first-order constraint — Li-ion gives **~1–4 h active runtime** (Figure 03 ~**2.3 kWh, ~3–4 h** ≈ half a shift); 24/7 needs hot-swap or staggered fleets. `✓`
**On-board compute:** **NVIDIA Jetson Thor** (Blackwell, ~**2,070 FP4 TFLOPS**, 128 GB, 40–130 W) — GA early 2026; named adopters include Agility, Boston Dynamics, Figure. `✓`
**Simulation / training:** **NVIDIA Isaac Lab** (GPU-parallel RL, ~4096 envs — the default for published Unitree H1/G1, Booster locomotion work); **MuJoCo Playground** (joystick locomotion on Berkeley Humanoid, Unitree G1, Booster T1; teleop hand-retarget + whole-body tracking); **NVIDIA Newton** physics engine (GA at GTC 2026, w/ DeepMind + Disney); Isaac Sim. `✓`
**Foundation-model stacks:** **NVIDIA Isaac GR00T** (GEAR lab) — lineage **N1** (open, GTC Mar 2025) → **N1.5** (frozen VLM + FLARE loss to learn from human video; tested on Fourier GR-1 + Unitree G1) → **N1.7** (open reasoning VLA, "commercially viable," vendor-reported) → **N2** (**previewed only**, DreamZero "world action model," "slated by end of year") `⏱`; **Figure Helix / Helix 02**; **Physical Intelligence π0 / π0.5** (flow-matching VLA, API/partnership only); **Skild Brain**. **Hugging Face LeRobot** for open humanoid data/policies `~ not re-fetched this pass`.

## 7. Trending & Notable `⏱ as of mid-2026`
**Humanoid foundation models are the headline** — NVIDIA GR00T (N1.7 shipping / N2 previewed), **Figure Helix 02** (vendor claim: first VLA to output high-rate *whole-upper-body* continuous control — wrists/torso/head/fingers — and to run one policy on two robots; a demo unloaded/reloaded a dishwasher in 61 sequential actions), **Physical Intelligence π0.5** (open-world mobile manipulation), **Skild Brain** ("omni-bodied" cross-embodiment). **RL whole-body sim-to-real** (Isaac Lab / MuJoCo Playground) is now the default locomotion recipe; **motion retargeting from human mocap/video** feeds imitation. The **teleoperation / data race** is the real 2026 story — collecting whole-body demonstrations is the moat, and it is also the tell.

**Counter-signals (mandatory — treat vendor demos as unproven until third-party-verified):**
- **Assume teleoperation unless a demo is explicitly labeled autonomous.** Morgan Stanley states this directly to investors, and warns of "the significant gap between market hype and scalable practical value." `✓ third-party`
- **Autonomy is the missing layer.** Independent surveys: platforms are "far from general-purpose autonomy"; **novel-object manipulation, fall recovery, unstructured environments, and multi-step reasoning without human intervention do not work yet.** Berkeley's **Ken Goldberg** frames a "~100,000-year data gap" between language data and real-world manipulation data. `✓ third-party`
- **1X NEO ships, but ~60–70% autonomous** — its "Expert Mode" hands control to a **remote human teleoperator** for the rest; a home robot that is partly a person on a headset. `~ third-party estimate`
- **Tesla Optimus:** ~**300** internal units in a "learning phase," **no commercial sales**, Gen 3 reveal slipped repeatedly through 2026. The most-marketed humanoid is not for sale. `✓ third-party`
- **Sanctuary AI pivoted off bespoke humanoid hardware** to selling its "Physical AI" as **software on third-party arms** — a direct read on how hard humanoid hardware commercialization is. `~`
- **Deployments are single-site / single-task.** GXO-Digit ≈ one Georgia facility; BMW-Figure ≈ one zone/task; "most-deployed" still means tens of robots, not fleets. `✓`
- **Reliability / uptime.** Industrial buyers want **95–99% uptime**; general-purpose humanoids don't hit it. Agility's cited **99.99%** reliability is for a **narrow, specific application**, not general-purpose. Battery caps continuous work at ~½ shift. `✓ third-party`
- **Gartner (Jan 2026, reported):** **fewer than ~20 companies worldwide** will scale humanoids **beyond pilots by 2028**. `~ reported`
- **Numbers vs press releases.** Morgan Stanley **~50,000** humanoids shipped in **2026** (revised up from ~28k) `✓ third-party`; Goldman Sachs (2024) **$38B TAM / ~1.4M shipments by 2035** — flagged as an *aggressive scenario*, not settled fact. Long-range figures ($5T by 2050) are scenarios, not forecasts.
- **China vs US.** Chinese firms were **~21 of 38** humanoid exhibitors at CES 2026; Morgan Stanley says China is "consolidating and expanding its lead" on manufacturing, and "US policy support is unlikely to reverse this trend in the short term." Note: as with quadrupeds, **US/EU security scrutiny** may curb Chinese platforms in sensitive Western sectors. `✓ third-party`
