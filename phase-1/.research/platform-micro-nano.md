## 1. Definition & Scope
Untethered robots from ~1 mm down to nanometers — magnetic microswimmers, helical/flagellar swimmers, drug-delivery capsules, cell/molecule manipulators, DNA-origami nanomachines. What makes it a distinct engineering target: **at this scale the physics inverts.** Surface forces beat body forces, inertia vanishes (**low-Reynolds**), Brownian noise intrudes, and there is **no room onboard for a battery, motor, computer, or sensor.** So the robot is *fabricated* (not assembled), *powered and steered by external fields* (magnetic, acoustic, optical, chemical) or by *living cells*, and *seen by external imaging* (X-ray, ultrasound, MRI, microscope). The entire control loop lives *off* the robot. This is the least CS-centric, most physics-and-fabrication-bound platform in the taxonomy — and `[★]` frontier because 2025–2026 is its clinical inflection. Two sub-forms: **micro** (~10 µm–1 mm, magnetic/acoustic, medical) and **nano** (<100 nm, DNA/enzyme, molecular delivery).

## 2. Signature Capability Stack
- **A10 Micro/Nano actuation & microfabrication** *(load-bearing — the platform's identity)* — scaling-law physics, field-driven micro-actuators, MEMS / two-photon / soft-litho fabrication. → `micro-nano-bio-robotics`
- **A4 External-field control** — closed-loop steering of underactuated, non-inertial bodies via coil-current / acoustic-frequency modulation; low-Re dynamics. → `control-classical-optimal-mpc`
- **A6 Learned control (RL) + sim-to-real** *(the frontier brain)* — RL for magnetic/ultrasound navigation and swarm exploration; PINN flow models bridge the sim-to-real gap. → `reinforcement-learning-control`, `sim-to-real-transfer`
- **A1 Imaging-guided micro-perception** — visual servoing under a microscope; X-ray/US/MRI/OCT tracking in vivo (the robot has no eyes of its own). → `computer-vision-3d-perception`
- **A2 Micro-localization** — pose/velocity estimation from external imaging. → `state-estimation-slam`
- **A9 Microswarm coordination** — collective magnetic control of thousands of agents; reconfigurable swarms. → `multi-robot-swarm`
- **Soft / continuum micro-bodies** — stimuli-responsive hydrogels, magnetic soft composites, slime/continuum. → `soft-robotics`
- **A8 Low-Reynolds simulation** — microfluidic CFD + PINNs for policy training. → `simulation-digital-twins`

## 3. Integration & Platform-Specific Skills
The "integration tax" no single capability owns: **microfabrication** (two-photon lithography / Nanoscribe, soft lithography / PDMS, template electrodeposition, Harvard pop-up PC-MEMS) — the manufacturing bottleneck; **external-field actuation-system engineering** — designing electromagnetic navigation systems (Helmholtz/Maxwell coil arrays, 8-coil OctoMag-class rigs), acoustic transducer arrays, optical tweezers; **low-Reynolds-number physics & scaling laws** (surface/capillary/adhesion forces, microfluidics); **medical-imaging-in-the-loop control** (steering by fluoroscopy/US/MRI with no onboard feedback); **biocompatibility, biodegradability & in-vivo translation** (materials, clearance, regulatory); and the defining constraint — **wireless everything: no onboard power, compute, or sensing.** Bio-hybrid variants add **living-cell actuator integration** (muscle- or bacteria-powered).

## 4. Typical Verticals
**B4 healthcare & surgical (dominant)** — targeted drug delivery, minimally-invasive endovascular/neuro intervention, thrombolysis (clot dissolution), precise payload release into hard-to-reach tissue; the entire commercial thesis. **Lab automation / life-science tooling** — single-cell/embryo/molecule manipulation, lab-on-chip, assay handling (Yu Sun–style robotic cell manipulation). **Emerging environmental** — microplastic/pollutant capture and water remediation. Chosen because only a sub-millimeter untethered agent can reach the vasculature, a single cell, or a capillary — the platform's unique reach *is* its value.

## 5. Eligibility Profile
The archetypal **"micro/nano robotics engineer"** *inverts* the usual CS-forward robotics weighting. Load-bearing backgrounds rise to first-class: **Mechanical Engineering** (microfabrication, MEMS, fluid mechanics — the dominant degree), **EE / Applied Physics** (electromagnetics, field actuation, MEMS readout), **Materials Science** (magnetic/stimuli-responsive/biodegradable materials), and **Bioengineering / BME / Chemistry** (bio-hybrid actuators, cell manipulation, biocompatibility). CS/AI is a *supporting* competency — central only to the learned-control, microswarm, and micro-vision sub-scopes; a CS-only profile without fabrication/physics/materials literacy is weak here. Platform must-haves: a **microfabrication/cleanroom skill** (two-photon or soft-litho), **electromagnetics / field-actuation**, **low-Re / microfluidics**, **imaging-guided control**. Feeder capability profiles: `micro-nano-bio-robotics`, `control-classical-optimal-mpc`, `reinforcement-learning-control`, `computer-vision-3d-perception`, `multi-robot-swarm`, `soft-robotics`. Tiers: **L1** fabrication/characterization technician → **L2–L3** microrobot control/vision + actuation-system engineer → **L4/L5** clinical-translation or learned-control researcher. Signal degrees: **ETH MAVT Microrobotics, EPFL Microengineering (ME-436), Max Planck IMPRS-IS, U Toronto MIE**. The premium: a fabricated, externally-steered micro/bio artifact with **closed-loop control** — ideally in a phantom or in vivo.

## 6. Platform-Specific Hardware & Stack `~`
**Actuation systems:** ETH **OctoMag** (8-electromagnet, 5-DOF eMNS) and mobile-coil rigs; **MagnebotiX** (MiniMag / MFG-100 electromagnetic manipulation, Zurich); **NanoFlex Robotics "Navion"** clinical eMNS; **Stereotaxis Genesis / GenesisX** (FDA-cleared robotic magnetic navigation) · Aeon Scientific · Magnetecs; Helmholtz/Maxwell coil arrays; acoustic transducers; optical tweezers. **Fabrication:** **Nanoscribe Photonic Professional GT2** two-photon lithography (IP-resists), soft lithography (PDMS), template electrodeposition / GLAD, Harvard pop-up PC-MEMS. **Materials:** iron-oxide/magnetite, NdFeB, tantalum (X-ray contrast), hydrogels, biodegradable polymers, artificial bacterial flagella. **Imaging (the "eyes"):** fluorescence microscopy, fluoroscopy/X-ray, ultrasound, MRI, OCT, photoacoustic. **Compute/software:** no onboard compute — control runs on an **external workstation/GPU**; Python/MATLAB + ROS 2 for macro rigs; COMSOL / custom low-Re CFD + **PINN** flow models; MuJoCo/Isaac + RL frameworks (e.g. the **Turbo** transformer policy) for sim-to-real. Reference robots: ETH **clinical microrobot** (gel + iron-oxide + tantalum), helical magnetic microswimmers, CUHK magnetic **microswarm/slime**, **Bionaut** rice-grain capsules, DNA-origami nanorobots.

## 7. Trending & Notable `⏱ as of mid-2026`
**Clinical inflection** — ETH/Swiss **clinically-ready magnetic microrobot** (*Science* 2025): soluble gel + iron-oxide (steer) + tantalum (X-ray), >95% delivery, demonstrated blood-clot dissolution; commercialized via ETH spinouts **NanoFlex Robotics** and **Swiss Vascular**. **Stereotaxis GenesisX** won FDA 510(k) clearance for endovascular robotic magnetic navigation. **Learned control crosses sim-to-real** — **Turbo** transformer-RL for microswarms in unknown environments (*Nature Machine Intelligence* 2026); model-based RL for **ultrasound-driven** microrobots (NMI 2025); RL navigation in simulated **blood capillaries** (arXiv 2606); PINN flow-aware steering. **Hybrid magneto-acoustic** actuation (CAMP, 2025) and enzyme-fueled nanobots. **Bio-hybrid** — MuMuTA muscle-tissue hand (*Science Robotics* 2025), Anthrobots/Neurobots. **DNA-nanorobot** cancer delivery advancing but still largely preclinical (in-vitro-dominant). *(Re-verify before publish.)*
