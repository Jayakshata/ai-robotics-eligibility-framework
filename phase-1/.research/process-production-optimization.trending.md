# Process & Production Optimization — Current Landscape (as of mid-2026)

**Niche scope:** the industrial-data-science layer that **tunes the PROCESS / PLANT** — process parameters, throughput, yield, and energy — via process modeling, Bayesian optimization, RL for process control, plant-level APC/MPC, digital-twin-driven optimization, surrogate/soft sensors, and energy analytics.

**Boundary discipline (MECE — reference, don't absorb):** this optimizes the *process*, not the robot. The robot's own control loop and RL-control live in **Axis A** (`control-classical-optimal-mpc`, `reinforcement-learning-control`) — cited here, not owned. Failure prediction / RUL is the sibling **Predictive Maintenance** niche; defect root-cause is **Quality/Yield Analytics**; demand/inventory is **Forecasting & Planning**. This profile's substance is **Bayesian optimization, hybrid/gray-box process models, surrogate & soft sensors, APC/MPC and RL at plant level, digital-twin optimization, and energy optimization.**

**Confidence:** claims are web-cited unless marked `~` (unverified / single-source) or *[assessment]*. Vendor performance numbers are labeled **vendor-reported**. Stamp: **mid-2026 (2026-07-10)**. `🆕` = 2025–26 release.

## TL;DR — what actually changed for this niche in 2025–26

- **Closed-loop AI process optimization became a named product category.** ARC Advisory Group created a **Closed Loop AI Optimization (AIO)** category; **Imubit's 🆕 Optimizing Brain** (deep-RL over a learned "Foundation Process Model," writing setpoints 24/7) is the reference product, and every APC incumbent now has an AI story. This is RL *at the plant/unit level* — distinct from robot RL-control.
- **The APC/MPC incumbents consolidated hard.** **🆕 Emerson completed its full acquisition of AspenTech (Mar 2025, ~$7.2B for the minority stake)** — the single biggest structural event for this niche, folding Aspen DMC3/DMCplus/GDOT APC into Emerson. **AVEVA (Schneider Electric)** and **Honeywell/Rockwell/Yokogawa** all pushed "autonomous operations" roadmaps.
- **Hybrid (gray-box) models — first-principles + ML — are the credible methodological center of gravity**, not black-box deep nets. They give the data-efficiency, extrapolation, and interpretability that process engineers require, and they underpin the modern digital twin.
- **Bayesian optimization matured into standard tooling** for process/recipe tuning and self-driving labs, consolidating onto **🆕 Meta's Ax 1.0 + BoTorch**.
- **Time-series foundation models arrived but are peripheral to *control*.** They matter for the forecasting/soft-sensing inputs (**🆕 Chronos-2**, Oct 2025, native multivariate+covariate); they do **not** replace MPC/RL for closed-loop process optimization. *[assessment]*
- **Generative AI entered the workflow as a natural-language layer over optimization**, not as the optimizer: Aspen **Virtual Advisor (AVA)** for DMC3, **Braincube Companion AI**, **Siemens Planning/Operations Copilot**.
- **Counter-signal is strong and niche-specific:** APC/MPC benefit **erosion** (models decay as the plant drifts) is a decades-old, well-documented failure mode — the real reason "autonomous plant" claims deserve skepticism — layered on top of the broad **RAND ~80% AI-project failure** and **McKinsey pilot-purgatory** statistics.

Market context (analyst-reported, wide ranges): the **digital-twin** market is cited at **~USD 36B (2025) → ~USD 180B (2030), ~38% CAGR** ([Pratiti](https://pratititech.com/10-digital-twin-use-cases-in-2026-for-manufacturing-energy-smart-buildings/)); **AI-in-energy** at **~USD 33–42B (2025–26)** growing ~20% CAGR ([Fortune Business Insights](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/ai-in-energy-market-117280)). Treat all such sizing as directional.

## 1. The methods / models frontier — what changed in 2025–26

Grouped by the four method families that actually define this niche.

### 1a. Reinforcement learning for process control (plant/unit level) — the headline shift
- **Closed-loop AI optimization / deep-RL over a learned process model** — the 2025–26 story. Learn a data-driven dynamic model of the unit, then use RL to drive setpoints to an economic objective inside constraints. *Why it matters:* it targets the multivariable, nonlinear, drifting problems that PID/APC handle poorly, and it closes the loop (writes setpoints), not just advises. Commercial form: **Imubit Optimizing Brain** ([Imubit](https://imubit.com/press-release/imubit-launches-optimizing-brain-solution-the-process-industrys-first-closed-loop-ai-optimization-solution-powered-by-reinforcement-learning/)). Deployed form: **Yokogawa FKDPP** (below).
- **Offline / safe RL** — the practical enabler, because online exploration on a live plant is unsafe. Learn a policy from historian data only. `🆕` **"Safe Deployment of Offline RL via Input-Convex Action Correction"** (input-convex NN cost model corrects unsafe actions at deployment, no retraining) — CSTR polymerization startup/grade-change case ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0098135425005381), [arXiv 2507.22640](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.22640)). `🆕` AIChE 2025: **offline RL for grade-change operations** in chemical plants ([AIChE](https://proceedings.aiche.org/conferences/aiche-annual-meeting/2025/proceeding/paper/offline-reinforcement-learning)).
- **Survey anchor:** `🆕` **"Recent Advances in Reinforcement Learning for Chemical Process Control"** (MDPI *Processes* 2025, 13(6):1791) — frames RL for controller tuning vs. direct policy learning, and the open problems: safety, data efficiency, and sim-to-real ([MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2227-9717/13/6/1791)).
- *Boundary note:* this is process-plant RL. The robot-embodied RL-control frontier (sim-to-real, VLAs) is Axis A `reinforcement-learning-control` — do not merge.

### 1b. Hybrid / gray-box process models (first-principles + ML) — the credible center
- **Hybrid ("gray-box") modeling** — combine mechanistic equations with ML that models only the residual/unknown terms. *Why it matters:* far better **extrapolation, interpretability, and data-efficiency** than black-box NNs — exactly the properties a process engineer needs to trust a model that will move a real valve. `🆕` step-by-step tutorial review ([ScienceDirect S0098135424003442](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0098135424003442)); hybrid framework for tuning control parameters ([ScienceDirect S1226086X24004623](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1226086X24004623)); unified deductive/inductive/abductive framework ([ScienceDirect S0098135425003989](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0098135425003989)).
- **Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) as soft sensors / surrogates** — enforce conservation laws / thermodynamics inside the network. *Why it matters:* virtual sensors for un-measurable variables and real-time surrogates that make optimization tractable. Examples: PINN **virtual thermal sensor** ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0017931023005380)); PINN surrogates for **adsorption / Direct Air Capture** processes ([MDPI Processes](https://doi.org/10.3390/pr13092824)).
- **ML-accelerated MPC & surrogate optimization** — learn a fast surrogate of an expensive first-principles model, then optimize/close the loop on the surrogate. Review: `🆕` **"Accelerating process control and optimization via machine learning: a review"** (covers ML-MPC, hybrid models, surrogate modeling, BO, RL) ([arXiv 2412.18529](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.18529)).

### 1c. Bayesian optimization & autonomous experimentation — for recipe/parameter tuning
- **Bayesian optimization (Gaussian-process surrogate + acquisition function)** — the data-efficient way to find optimal process parameters when each trial is expensive. *Why it matters:* replaces brute-force DoE; the core method for tuning setpoints, recipes, and formulations. 2025–26 advances: **active-constraint-learning BO** for feasible manufacturing windows ([Taylor & Francis](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/24725854.2025.2475505)); **contextual BO to cut carbon footprint** (reported 26% CO₂ / 22% cost reduction on FDM, *paper-reported*) ([Springer IJAMT](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00170-025-16110-9)); robustness study of Bayesian experimental design in production engineering ([Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/manufacturing-technology/articles/10.3389/fmtec.2025.1614335/full)).
- **Self-driving labs (SDLs) / autonomous experimentation** — BO "brain" + automated hardware closing the experiment loop. *Why it matters:* the emerging pattern for process/formulation development; the **Acceleration Consortium** curates the field ([awesome-self-driving-labs](https://github.com/AccelerationConsortium/awesome-self-driving-labs)), and **Atlas** is a representative SDL optimization brain ([RSC Digital Discovery](https://pubs.rsc.org/en/content/articlehtml/2025/dd/d4dd00115j)).

### 1d. Time-series foundation models — relevant to inputs, not to closed-loop control
- **🆕 Amazon Chronos-2** (arXiv 2510.15821, **Oct 2025**) — first mature TSFM with **native univariate + multivariate + covariate** zero-shot forecasting via a "group attention" mechanism. *Why it matters here:* forecasting feeds/loads/energy prices and multi-sensor soft-sensing — the *inputs* to optimization — but it is a forecaster, not a controller ([Amazon Science](https://www.amazon.science/blog/introducing-chronos-2-from-univariate-to-universal-forecasting), [DeepLearning.AI](https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch/amazons-chronos-2-sorts-out-tangled-variables-to-make-better-predictions)).
- **🆕 Google TimesFM 2.5** — ~200M-param decoder-only, tops **GIFT-Eval**; vendor/blog materials claim large PdM ROI (**blog-reported**) ([MachineLearningMastery](https://machinelearningmastery.com/the-2026-time-series-toolkit-5-foundation-models-for-autonomous-forecasting/)). **Salesforce Moirai-2 / MOIRAI-MoE** — any-variate, built for messy multi-sensor data. **Nixtla TimeGPT** — managed API entry point (`~`TimeGPT-2.1 / Enterprise 2.0 add multivariate + "agentic forecasting" per Nixtla).
- **Reality check `~`:** TSFMs are trained on web/finance/ecology data and **lack grounding in physical actuation** — they struggle with the interacting, physically-constrained sensor streams of a plant. For *control*, hybrid models + MPC/RL remain the substance. `🆕` **FactoryNet** is an early attempt at a large industrial-time-series corpus to close this gap (`~`arXiv 2605.09081).

## 4. Benchmarks & evaluation practice

- **Tennessee Eastman Process (TEP)** — the de-facto benchmark for process control, monitoring, and fault diagnosis; complex multivariate nonlinear dynamics. Used for **offline-RL process optimization** (e.g., KAUST behavior-cloning + value-learning policy) and for ML fault-detection benchmarking; **extended/expanded TEP datasets** are now standard ([KAUST](https://repository.kaust.edu.sa/items/decfab4b-67eb-40c4-8cfb-75369ad974a3), [ScienceDirect extended-TEP](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0098135421000594), [`🆕`ChemRxiv 2026 ML-FD benchmark](https://chemrxiv.org/doi/10.26434/chemrxiv.10001628)).
- **🆕 PC-Gym** (Computers & Chemical Engineering, 2025) — purpose-built **RL benchmark environments for process control** (CSTR, multistage extraction, crystallizer) with constraints, disturbances, and — crucially — a **nonlinear-MPC oracle** to score RL against. This is the niche-specific evaluation harness ([PC-Gym](https://maximilianb2.github.io/pc-gym/), [arXiv 2410.22093](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.22093), [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0098135425003667)).
- **GIFT-Eval** (Salesforce) — the leaderboard for **time-series foundation-model forecasting** (28 datasets, 144k series, 7 domains incl. Energy); how the TSFM *inputs* to optimization get compared ([Salesforce](https://www.salesforce.com/blog/gift-eval-time-series-benchmark/), [HF leaderboard](https://huggingface.co/spaces/Salesforce/GIFT-Eval)).
- **Evaluation practice specific to this niche:** don't score a controller on prediction error — score it on **economic objective, constraint-violation rate, and closed-loop stability vs. an MPC oracle** (PC-Gym's design encodes exactly this). For optimization campaigns: **regret / trials-to-target** vs. DoE baselines. For deployed APC: **benefit capture and its decay over time** (see §5).

## 5. Counter-signals (the load-bearing skepticism)

- **APC/MPC benefit *erosion* — the niche-specific, decades-documented failure mode.** APC performance **degrades as the plant drifts** (equipment changes, feed/quality shifts, instrument drift, operators dropping constrained variables); "industry has experienced high maintenance and degraded performance of many MPC applications" ([ISA InTech](https://www.isa.org/intech-home/2019/january-february/features/advanced-process-control-process-optimization-tool), [CruxOCM](https://cruxocm.com/advanced-process-control/)). *Implication:* the honest value proposition of "AI/adaptive" APC is often **sustaining** benefit against erosion, not a headline one-time gain — and it is the real reason "autonomous plant" claims deserve scrutiny.
- **RL-for-process-control is not production-default.** Safety of online exploration, data efficiency, and sim-to-real are unresolved; the field is explicitly steering to *offline* RL with safety layers precisely because live exploration is unacceptable ([MDPI review](https://www.mdpi.com/2227-9717/13/6/1791)). `~`arXiv work on "challenges of RL for controlling industrial energy systems" and "trustworthy/explainable deep RL for compressed-air systems" catalogs the same gaps.
- **Broad AI-project failure rates** (context, enterprise-wide, not niche-specific): **RAND ~80.3%** of enterprise AI projects fail to deliver promised value (late 2025); **McKinsey** — nearly two-thirds stuck in "pilot mode"; **IDC/Lenovo** — ~4 of every 33 POCs reach production; **Gartner** — poor **data quality** is the leading root cause ([mybusinessfuture/RAND-Gartner](https://mybusinessfuture.com/en/80-ai-failure-rate-2026-how-rand-and-gartner-expose-the-ai/), [Astrafy](https://astrafy.io/the-hub/blog/technical/scaling-ai-from-pilot-purgatory-why-only-33-reach-production-and-how-to-beat-the-odds)). For this niche, the binding constraint is usually **the data backbone** (bad/missing historian data, no UNS) — which is why plumbing is the prerequisite investment.
- **TSFMs lack physical grounding** — trained on non-industrial data, they mishandle interacting, constrained sensor streams; for control they are not a substitute for hybrid models + MPC/RL. *[assessment, backed by FactoryNet motivation and TimesFM industrial write-ups]*
- **Vendor ROI claims are vendor-reported.** Imubit (>20% utilization), Rockwell (kiln $330K/yr, dairy 42%/6–15.8%), DeepMind (40% cooling), TimesFM (10–30× ROI) are all self- or blog-reported — cite as such, not as independent benchmarks.

## Net trend (synthesis for §7 of the profile)

Classical **APC/MPC still runs production** process optimization; the 2025–26 growth edge is **closed-loop AI optimization (offline/safe RL over learned or hybrid models)** and **Bayesian optimization for parameter/recipe tuning**, with **hybrid gray-box models** as the trusted modeling substrate and **digital twins** as the integration surface. **Time-series foundation models** matter for the *forecasting/soft-sensing inputs*, not for closed-loop control. **Generative AI** is entering as a **natural-language interface** over the optimizer, not as the optimizer. The enabling investment underneath everything is the **data backbone (UNS: OPC UA + MQTT/Sparkplug B) and modern historians**. The credible skeptic's frame is **benefit erosion** and **data-quality/pilot-purgatory** — treat vendor ROI as vendor-reported and score controllers economically against an **MPC oracle (PC-Gym)**, not on prediction error.
