## 1. Definition & Scope
> 2–4 sentences: what this capability is, why it matters, in-scope vs. out-of-scope, where it sits.

## 2. Role Archetypes & Seniority Tiers
> The job ladder. L1→L4/L5 with a one-line mandate each.

## 3. Eligibility Criteria — Acceptable & Highly-Valued Credentials
> Named, not generic. Grounded in `_grounding-reference.md` + niche-specific research.
> - **Acceptable undergraduate degrees** (fields; mark which are strongest for THIS niche)
> - **Highly-valued undergraduate programs** (named, research-backed)
> - **Acceptable postgraduate degrees** (fields/specializations)
> - **Highly-valued postgraduate programs** (named; ranked by strength for THIS niche)
> - **Highly-valued certifications** (named; niche-relevant)
> - **Experience & portfolio** (per tier)

## 4. Skill Profile (JD-style)
> Write skills the way a **LinkedIn job description** reads — scannable bullets, hireable-looking, NOT prose. Three blocks:
> - **Required / must-have skills** — the concrete competencies any engineer in this niche needs.
> - **Preferred / differentiating skills** — what sets a strong candidate apart (often the frontier items).
> - **Tools & tech stack** — named frameworks, libraries, platforms, hardware.
> Optionally note how the bar rises by tier. This is the practical "what you can DO" list; §5 is the academic "what you studied" list.

## 5. Core Subject List
> Explicit subjects/modules relevant to this niche, grounded in real program syllabi (note which programs teach each). Group: **Foundational maths & CS · Core robotics · Niche-specific · Frontier/advanced.** A flat catalog, not a sequence.

## 6. Training Programme Design & Duration (optional build)
> If we build our own programme: module structure + **duration in months** (hours/week). Total + per-module. Two intensities — **Fast-track** (full-time) and **Thorough** (part-time). Table: module → months → hours → outcome. Anchor to `_grounding-reference.md` §D.

## 7. Trending Research Topics (current) `⏱`
> The currently-hot research topics, models, methods, and named systems in this niche — the equivalent of **SAM2 / DINO** in vision. Group by theme; **name specific models/papers/systems**; one line each on what it is and why it matters.
> **⏱ TIME-SENSITIVE:** web-verify and refresh at publish; stamp with an **"as of <month year>"** date. This section is what keeps the programme's frontier current, and it dates fastest — flag it for periodic re-research.

## 8. Hands-On Labs & Projects
> Catalog grouped by difficulty (foundational / applied / advanced / capstone) — what each teaches, what "done" looks like. A catalog, not a timed roadmap.

## 9. Required Lab Setup / Hardware / Resources
> Robots/platforms (named) · sensors/end-effectors · compute · software/simulators · datasets & benchmarks.

## 10. Partnerships & Ecosystem
> Hardware vendors · sim/compute providers · academic labs · open-source communities · industry programs · standards/certification bodies.

## 11. Assessment & Validation
> Objective validation: knowledge checks, project rubrics, standardized benchmarks, portfolio/system-design review, recognized certifications as evidence.

## 12. Adjacent & Related Niches
> Which capabilities pair with this, which platform tracks (C) and verticals (B) it feeds. Reference only — no career roadmap.
