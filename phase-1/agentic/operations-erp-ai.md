# Per-Niche Profile — Operations, ERP & Manufacturing-Execution AI

> **Applied-AI area profile (Agentic & Applied AI, Area 1).** The agentic software layer *over operations* — intelligent ERP/MES copilots, production-scheduling agents, supply-chain orchestration agents, and agentic RPA that reason in language and act through tools (ERP/MES/WMS/TMS APIs, OR solvers, RPA bots) — NOT the statistical demand forecast or scheduling optimizer underneath (that is Area 2). Research-backed (mid-2026). `✓` grounded · `~` to-verify · `⏱` time-sensitive.

**Draws on:** LLM/agent/RAG software engineering **+** operations/ERP/supply-chain domain literacy · **Serves platforms (C):** platform-agnostic (enterprise/MES layer above the fleet) · **Verticals (B):** B1 manufacturing (MES, MRP, scheduling), B2 logistics & supply chain

## 1. Definition & Scope
Building **AI copilots and agents that assist and automate operations** — an "intelligent ERP" copilot, an MES/shop-floor agent, a production-scheduling agent, a supply-chain orchestration agent, or agentic RPA that reasons in natural language and acts through tools (ERP/MES/WMS/TMS APIs, OR solvers, RPA bots). This is the **agentic layer over the system of record**: the agent *reads* the demand forecast, *calls* the scheduling solver, and *pushes* a purchase order or schedule change back into the ERP — under guardrails and human approval. Because it **acts on business state** (inventory, POs, schedules, money), it must respect MRP/MRP-II logic, S&OP cadence, the ISA-95 ERP↔MES boundary, and audit/governance — which makes the operations-domain moat the sharpest differentiator in all of Area 1 (a domain-naïve LLM engineer will let an agent release a bad PO). **In scope:** LLM/agent orchestration over transactional systems, RAG over ops data, tool-use into ERP/MES/WMS/solvers, agentic RPA, evals for state-mutating actions, human-in-the-loop approval. **Out of scope:** the statistical demand forecast / demand-sensing model and the scheduling-optimization solver themselves (Area 2 — the agent *reads and calls* them, it does not build them), and training the foundation models (you *consume* Claude/GPT/Gemini).

## 2. Role Archetypes & Seniority Tiers
| Tier | Title | Mandate |
|------|-------|---------|
| **L1** | Junior / Applied-AI Engineer | Builds RAG over ops data + simple tool-using agents under supervision (e.g. answers "why is this order late?" by reading an ERP/WMS API); runs evals; integrates LLM APIs. |
| **L2** | AI / Agentic Engineer (Supply-Chain / Industrial AI) | Owns one ops copilot/agent end-to-end integrating a real ERP/MES/planning system or OR solver (RAG + tool-use + evals + human-in-the-loop for write actions); debugs retrieval quality, cost, latency, guardrails. |
| **L3** | Senior Applied-AI Engineer | Architects the multi-agent ops platform (orchestration, memory, the MCP/tool surface into transactional systems); sets the eval + guardrail strategy for actions that mutate business state; respects the ISA-95 boundary; mentors. |
| **L4/L5** | Staff / Principal AI Engineer — Industrial/Operations Copilots | Sets platform direction; advances agentic patterns for safety- and audit-critical operations; owns reliability, governance (AIGP/ISO-42001-grade) and ROI; interfaces with the Area-2 forecasting/OR teams whose outputs the agents consume. |

## 3. Eligibility Criteria — Acceptable & Highly-Valued Credentials
**Software-first role `✓`:** the gate is **software engineering + demonstrable LLM/agent/RAG/eval ability**; operations/ERP/supply-chain domain literacy is the **differentiator that stacks on top** — and for *this* niche it is the sharpest differentiator in all of Area 1, because an ops agent takes autonomous actions on inventory, POs, schedules and money, so it must respect MRP logic, the ISA-95 ERP↔MES boundary, and audit/governance (the ideal hire is "a strong AI engineer who learns the operations/ERP domain," not "a supply-chain planner who dabbles in LLMs"). Nobody needs kinematics or control.

**Acceptable undergraduate degrees** — Tier 1: **Computer Science / Software Engineering**, **AI / ML / Data Science** — the role is ~80% distributed-systems/backend software "with a probabilistic component" (ship reliable, observable, cost-controlled services that call transactional systems). Tier 2: **Industrial & Systems Engineering (ISyE) / Operations Research**, **Management Information Systems**, EE/ECE. **Domain differentiator (with SWE skills):** **Supply-Chain / Operations Management / Manufacturing / Industrial Engineering** — someone who understands S&OP, MRP/MRP-II, the ISA-95 boundary and how an SAP/Oracle/Dynamics process actually runs builds agents that stay grounded, auditable and safe to give write-access. The bullseye is the rare double: a CS grad with OR/supply-chain coursework, or an ISyE grad who is genuinely a strong software engineer.

**Highly-valued programs / courses `✓`** — no dedicated "ops/ERP AI-agent" degree; strength = CS-with-LLM/agent depth for the base **plus** a named supply-chain/OR program for the domain moat. *Base:* Stanford (**CS224N** NLP, **CS336** build-an-LLM, **CS25** Transformers), **UC Berkeley's free "LLM Agents" MOOC** (Dawn Song), CMU **MSAII/MSML**, Georgia Tech **OMSCS** (Generative-AI/LLM track, very low cost), UIUC MCS; the de-facto textbook is **Chip Huyen, *AI Engineering* (O'Reilly, 2025)**. *Domain moat:* the **MIT Supply-Chain Management MicroMasters** (MITx — the most name-brand, stackable, remote domain credential, with a credit pathway into MIT's SCM master's) and **Georgia Tech ISyE** (the #1-ranked ISyE — **MS Supply Chain Engineering**, **MS Operations Research**, the strongest bridge to the solver/optimization side an ops agent orchestrates); ~**MIT ORC / LGO** for elite OR/operations.

**Acceptable postgraduate degrees** — MS in CS/AI/ML (valued, not required), or the differentiated domain path — **MS Operations Research / ISyE / Supply-Chain Engineering** (Georgia Tech ISyE, MIT ORC/SCM/LGO) paired with a strong software portfolio. A PhD is not expected (this is applied engineering, not ML research); an OR PhD is a bonus only where the niche edges into optimization-heavy scheduling. **Distinctive `✓`: a shipped ops agent that safely writes to a real ERP/MES/planning system — with eval metrics + HITL approval — outweighs an extra degree** (eval design over transactional systems is the single biggest signal the person actually built with LLMs). The graduate degree is the fastest way to *acquire* the domain moat for someone who lacks it — not a gate.

**Highly-valued certifications `✓`** — three stacks. *Base agent/LLM/cloud (shared with Area 1):* **DeepLearning.AI *Agentic AI*** + *AI Agents in LangGraph*; **Hugging Face *AI Agents Course*** (free + certified); **NVIDIA NCA-GENL** / **Agentic AI (Professional)**; **IBM RAG & Agentic AI**; **Databricks Certified GenAI Engineer**; a cloud cert (**AWS AI Practitioner** / **MLA-C01**, **Azure AI-102→AI-103**, **Google PMLE**). *ERP / automation-platform (the "which systems can your agent act inside" signal):* **Microsoft AB-100 Agentic AI Business Solutions Architect** and **AB-620 AI Agent Builder Associate** (Copilot Studio, Dynamics 365, A2A+MCP; note **PL-500 RPA Developer retired 2026-06-30** `⏱`, with Microsoft's explicit position that "RPA as a standalone practice is converging with agentic AI"); **SAP C_AIG_2412 GenAI Developer** (build agents *inside* SAP via Joule Studio); **Oracle Fusion Cloud SCM** paths; **UiPath Agentic Automation Associate/Professional** (the agentic-RPA moat). *Operations-domain moat:* **ASCM/APICS CPIM** (no prerequisites — the single most on-point cert for a scheduling/MES/MRP agent; it teaches the exact MRP/inventory logic the agent must respect), **CSCP**, **CLTD**; **ISA-95 / IEC 62264 Enterprise-Control System Integration** (ISA — the MES↔ERP boundary moat); governance **IAPP AIGP** and **ISO/IEC 42001** (load-bearing here — ops agents take autonomous financial/inventory actions). Certs supplement a portfolio; they don't replace a shipped agent.

**Experience & portfolio** — L1: a RAG assistant over ops data + a tool-using agent, with eval numbers; no domain cert required (a CPIM-in-progress or a MITx SCM course is a plus). L2: 2–4 yr, a **shipped ops copilot** integrating a real ERP/MES/planning system or OR solver, with evals + HITL approval for write actions. L3: 5–8 yr, architected a multi-agent ops platform to production. L4/L5: 8+ yr and/or notable applied-agent work; owns governance/ROI. `~` 2026 US Agentic-AI-Engineer base band cited at ~$185k–$320k with growth-stage equity.

## 4. Skill Profile (JD-style)

**Required / must-have**
- Strong **Python** (incl. async); REST APIs (**FastAPI**), Git, Docker, a cloud, SQL — the role is ~80% reliable, observable, cost-controlled backend that calls transactional systems
- **LLM app development** — prompt engineering, **tool-use / function-calling**, structured outputs, LLM APIs (Claude, GPT, Gemini)
- **RAG over ops data** — chunking/parsing, embeddings, **vector DBs**, **hybrid search (BM25 + vector)**, reranking, retrieval-relevance evaluation
- **Agents / orchestration** — **LangGraph / LangChain**; multi-agent patterns; state & memory; **MCP** tool-calling into ERP/WMS/MES/solvers
- **Actions with side-effects** — **human-in-the-loop / approval gates** on write actions, idempotency & rollback, **guardrails** (prompt-injection, excessive agency, least-privilege tool scope; OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications)
- **Evals & observability** — golden sets, **trajectory-level eval** (not just final-answer), LLM-as-judge; tracing (LangSmith / Langfuse / Braintrust / Phoenix)
- **Production** — streaming, caching, **cost & latency** optimization (hybrid SLM + frontier routing)

**Preferred / differentiating**
- The **solver-as-tool** pattern — the agent calls an OR solver (**OR-Tools / Gurobi**) or a planning engine and *explains* the result; it does not replace the optimizer
- **Process Intelligence** grounding (Celonis-style event-log mining) so the agent knows how the business *actually* runs, not how it's documented
- Agentic RPA — the **agent-in-front-of-bot** pattern: reason on messy input → hand validated, structured output to an RPA bot that executes on the legacy system
- **Domain (the moat):** S&OP, **MRP / MRP-II**, inventory & lead-time policy, order-to-cash / procure-to-pay, the **ISA-95 ERP↔MES** boundary and its data objects, and how an SAP/Oracle/Dynamics/Kinaxis/Blue Yonder/o9 process runs — so an agent stays grounded, auditable and safe to give write-access

**Tools & tech stack** — *Languages:* Python (+ TypeScript) · *Agents:* LangGraph/LangChain, **Claude Agent SDK**, **Microsoft Agent Framework**, CrewAI, smolagents; **MCP + A2A** · *ERP/ops platforms (act inside):* SAP **Joule Studio**, Microsoft **Copilot Studio** / Dynamics 365, Oracle **AI Agent Studio**, **Kinaxis Maestro**, **UiPath Maestro**, ServiceNow, **IBM watsonx Orchestrate** · *RAG:* pgvector/Qdrant/Weaviate, hybrid search, rerankers (Cohere/Voyage/Jina) · *Solvers (as tools):* OR-Tools, Gurobi · *Evals:* LangSmith, Braintrust, Langfuse, Phoenix · *Models:* Claude (Fable 5 / Opus 4.8), GPT-5.5, Gemini 3.1 Pro; **DeepSeek V4** for on-prem/air-gapped · *Runtime:* Amazon Bedrock AgentCore

**Bar by tier** — L1: builds RAG + a tool-agent over ops data with evals · L2: ships an agent that safely writes to a real ERP/MES, fluent across RAG→agent→eval→HITL→deploy · L3: architects the multi-agent ops platform, deep in ≥1 area (retrieval, action-governance, or ERP integration) · L4/L5: sets platform direction, owns reliability/governance/ROI.

## 6. Training Programme Design & Duration `✓`
Software-conversion programme — like the sibling copilots track, **faster than the embodied tracks** for someone already coding (no kinematics/hardware core); ~1,190 hours. It runs a touch longer than the read-only copilots track because two things are heavier: **actions-with-side-effects governance** (an agent that writes to the ERP is a financial actor) and the **operations-domain moat** (CPIM/ISA-95-grade). Phase 4 = the ops/ERP/MES domain; phase 5 = ship a state-mutating agent with HITL.

| Phase | Focus | Fast-track | Thorough | ~Hrs | Outcome |
|-------|-------|-----------|----------|------|---------|
| 1. Foundations | Python/SWE, ML/NLP basics, transformers | 1.5 mo | 5 mo | 250 | Build & ship a Python service |
| 2. LLM & agent core | Prompting, tool-use, agent patterns, MCP | 1.5 mo | 4 mo | 240 | A working tool-using agent |
| 3. Agentic ops & production | RAG over ops data, actions-with-side-effects/HITL, trajectory evals, agentic security | 2.0 mo | 5 mo | 280 | A grounded, evaluated, guardrailed agent |
| 4. **Domain specialization** | ERP/MES/MRP + ISA-95 + solver-as-tool; integrate via MCP | 2.0 mo | 4–5 mo | 240 | The niche core |
| 5. Capstone + portfolio | Ship an ops agent that safely writes to a real ERP/MES/planning system with evals + HITL | 1.0–1.5 mo | 3–4 mo | 180 | Job-ready evidence |
| **Total** | | **≈ 8–10 months** (full-time) | **≈ 20–26 months** (part-time) | ~1,190 | Junior applied-AI / ops-agent engineer |

*For an existing software engineer, phases 2–4 (the LLM/agent + action-governance + operations-domain layer) compress to ~3.5–5 months; an ISyE/OR/supply-chain professional who can already code inverts the effort — they own phase 4 and spend the time on phases 1–3.*

## 7. Trending Research Topics `⏱ as of mid-2026`
> **Time-sensitive — re-research on a schedule.** `🆕` = 2025–26 release.
- **The category shift — "system of record → system of action":** the defining 2025–26 move is from assistive AI (alert/suggest/chat) to **agentic AI that plans, decides and executes multi-step work inside business systems** under guardrails (contact supplier → negotiate spot freight → reroute → log to ERP). **🆕 Frontier LLMs ship "extended thinking by default"** — Claude **Opus 4.8 / Fable 5**, **GPT-5.5**, **Gemini 3.1 Pro**, **Grok 4.3**; **DeepSeek V4** (+ V4-Flash) on-prem — with teams running a **hybrid stack** (cheap local/SLM for the routine 70–80%, frontier API for the hard 20–30%).
- **Horizontal agent frameworks (hit stable 1.0):** **🆕 LangGraph 1.0 / LangChain 1.0** (GA Oct 2025 — durable state, built-in persistence, first-class HITL; the production-auditable default) · **🆕 Microsoft Agent Framework 1.0** (GA Apr 2026 — merges AutoGen + Semantic Kernel, which go to maintenance mode) · thin tier: **Claude Agent SDK** (subagent context isolation), Strands (AWS), CrewAI, LlamaIndex Workflows, Pydantic AI, smolagents. Runtime: **🆕 Amazon Bedrock AgentCore** (GA Apr 2026 — framework-agnostic; agents run autonomously up to ~8 hrs with auto-scaling + governance).
- **Interop protocols (load-bearing, now governed):** **MCP** (agent→tool; ~18k+ community servers `~`) and **A2A** (agent↔agent; 150+ orgs), both now under the **🆕 Linux Foundation Agentic AI Foundation** (Dec 2025) — a neutral standard that de-risks building on it; consensus stack **MCP + A2A + WebMCP**. This is how an ops agent exposes "the ERP / WMS / supplier API / MES / solver" as callable tools.
- **Native agent studios in every ERP/ops suite (all 🆕):** **SAP Joule Studio** (GA Q1 2026; 40+ prebuilt agents, ~2,400 "Joule Skills" `~` `[vendor-reported]`) · **Microsoft Copilot Studio** + Dynamics 365 agents · **Oracle AI Agent Studio for Fusion** · **Kinaxis Maestro Agent Studio** · **UiPath Maestro** · **ServiceNow AI Agent Studio** (governed by AI Control Tower) · **Celonis AgentC** (Process-Intelligence-grounded) · **IBM watsonx Orchestrate** (prebuilt Finance + Supply-Chain agents) — build agents *inside* the suite that owns the data.
- **Live research edge:** **🆕 agentic memory & long-horizon execution** (Agentic Memory, Memory-as-Action, IterResearch, MemRL/Memory-R1 — an order-to-cash or month-end-close agent fails on **context explosion**, not single-step accuracy) · **🆕 LLM-planner + constraint-solver scheduling hybrids** (**A4PS** as the academic anchor, `~`iFactory-style RL-policy-+-solver 15-min replans off live ERP/MES — the LLM produces a candidate + explains it, a solver guarantees feasibility) · **Process Intelligence as the grounding layer** (Celonis: "no AI without PI").
- **Evals & security (the discipline that separates a demo from prod):** **🆕 τ²-bench** (Sierra — state-verifying, dual-control) and **🆕 CRMArena-Pro** (Salesforce — multi-turn enterprise; leading models ~58% single-turn dropping to ~35% multi-turn) quantify the reliability gap; **🆕 DynaSchedBench** for scheduling agents · **trajectory-level eval** (final-answer-only eval passes 20–40% more cases than trajectory eval reveals — it *lies*) · **🆕 OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications (2026)** (prompt injection, excessive agency, sensitive-data disclosure, unbounded consumption).

*Net trend: high-leverage back-office automation moving from copilot to autonomous action — but the counter-signal is loud and credible: Gartner projects **>40% of agentic-AI projects cancelled by end-2027** (plus "agent-washing" — only ~130 of thousands of "agentic" vendors are judged real), MIT NANDA found **~95% of enterprise GenAI pilots deliver no measurable P&L impact**, and τ-bench/CRMArena-Pro show frontier agents failing **>50%** of realistic enterprise tasks and degrading sharply in multi-turn. Treat every vendor ROI / GA / "autonomous-resolution" percentage as vendor-reported; the honest framing is "high-leverage ops automation that still keeps a human on high-value actions," not "autonomous enterprise."*

## 11. Assessment & Validation
- **Eval metrics** as portable evidence: retrieval recall, **task success at the trajectory level** (not just final-answer), **state-correctness** after a write (τ-bench-style DB checks), cost/latency, and **HITL-intervention rate** on high-value actions.
- **Portfolio:** a **shipped ops agent that safely writes to a real ERP/MES/planning system** + a system-design interview (multi-agent architecture, the ISA-95 boundary, action-governance, cost) + live debugging (a retrieval-failure, a prompt-injection, or an unsafe-write case).
- **Domain-governance check (niche-specific):** does the agent respect MRP/S&OP logic and the ISA-95 ERP↔MES boundary, keep a human on high-value actions, and stay **auditable** — every state-mutating call traceable and reversible?
