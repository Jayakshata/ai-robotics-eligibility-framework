## 1. Definition & Scope
Building the **systems and processes that govern, assure and secure other AI models** — model governance & risk management (**NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001**), regulatory compliance (**EU AI Act**), **alignment, red-teaming and safety evaluation**, and **AI-system security** (prompt injection, jailbreaks, model/data supply-chain). This is the layer that acts **on the AI itself**: it makes a model's safety and compliance claims **falsifiable and auditable** — building eval harnesses, guardrails, red-team suites and conformity packages — it does **not** drive any robot. The niche splits into two hireable tracks that share a spine: **Track A — technical AI-safety/assurance engineering** (software-first: evals, red-teaming, guardrails, alignment) and **Track B — AI governance/risk/compliance (GRC)** (framework + audit fluency). **In scope:** governance of the **software models** — EU AI Act / NIST AI RMF / ISO 42001 conformity, alignment, red-teaming, safety evals, prompt-injection and AI-supply-chain security. **Out of scope:** **robot functional safety** (ISO 10218 / ISO 13849 — the machine's e-stops and safety-rated controllers), which is the Axis-A `safety-verification-assurance` capability; and training the underlying foundation models (out of framework scope — you *govern* Claude/GPT/Gemini, you don't build them).

| Tier | Title | Mandate |
|------|-------|---------|
| **L1** | Junior AI Safety / Eval Engineer · AI Policy / Governance Analyst | **A:** runs model evals & red-team probes under supervision, builds a guardrail or jailbreak test suite. **B:** maintains the AI inventory/risk register, maps controls to a framework. |
| **L2** | AI Safety / AI Security Engineer · AI Governance Analyst | **A:** owns a model's eval + guardrail + red-team stack end-to-end; designs human + automated evals for non-deterministic behaviour. **B:** owns an EU-AI-Act high-risk conformity workflow or an ISO-42001 / NIST-RMF control set for a product line. |
| **L3** | Senior / Staff AI Safety Engineer · Senior Responsible-AI / AI-Governance Manager | **A:** designs the org's evaluation & red-team **strategy**; leads alignment-mitigation for a model family; sets AI-security standards. **B:** runs the AIMS program; ISO-42001 Lead Auditor / ISACA territory; owns a product line's risk posture. |
| **L4/L5** | Principal AI Safety / Head of Model Assurance · Head of Responsible AI / AI Risk Officer | Owns **model-release governance & safety-case direction** (A) or **EU-AI-Act conformity, regulator relationships & board reporting** (B). |

## 3. Eligibility Criteria — Acceptable & Highly-Valued Credentials
**Two-track role, software-first spine `✓`:** the framework's gate — **software engineering + demonstrable LLM/eval/red-team ability** — is exactly **Track A** (the assurance engineer who *builds* the evals, red-team harnesses and guardrails; it is "a software-engineering job with a probabilistic component"). **Track B** (governance/risk/compliance) is the **one Area-1 niche where law/public-policy is a genuinely competitive entry path** — but it gates on **regulatory-framework fluency + a certification**, not code. The strongest single profile is **CS/ML + regulatory literacy** — a coder who can read the EU AI Act — because that person can both *implement* the assurance and *attest* to it. Robotics/manufacturing domain knowledge is the **differentiator stacked on top**, not the entry ticket; nobody needs kinematics or control.

**Acceptable undergraduate degrees `✓`** — from a 146-posting governance-market study (fields: CS 29%, Data Science 22%, Info Systems 7%, Engineering 6%, Law 6%, Business 4%, Statistics/Risk/Cyber 3% each; 66% require a degree). **Tier 1 (Track-A spine):** **Computer Science / Software Engineering**, **AI / ML / Data Science** (CS+DS alone are ~51% of postings and the only auto-qualifying path into Track A). **Tier 1 for Track B only:** **Law / Public Policy / Philosophy (ethics)** — first-class for governance/compliance and dominant at head-of-governance level; small in volume (~6%) but the niche's distinctive credential signal. **Tier 2 (with technical evidence):** **Cybersecurity / Information Systems**, EE/ECE, Statistics/Maths — strongest for the AI-security sub-specialty (prompt-injection, supply-chain, MITRE ATLAS). **Domain differentiator:** a robotics/manufacturing engineering background that lets you map an ML safety function to its regulatory classification (the moat, in §4).

**Highly-valued programs / courses `✓`** — no dedicated undergrad "Responsible-AI" degree; strength = a CS/ML degree + named AI-safety/governance courses, or a master's-level ethics/governance programme. **Stanford is the deepest:** **CS 120** (Introduction to AI Safety), **CS 329T** (Trustworthy ML), **CS 281** (Ethics of AI), **STS 14 / CS 134** (Introduction to AI Governance), plus **CS 329H** (ML from Human Preferences / RLHF) and **CS 521** (AI-Safety seminar) `~` *(confirm current-year offering ⏱)*. **Closest thing to a "degree for the niche" (master's-level):** **Cambridge MSt in AI Ethics and Society** (Leverhulme CFI; part-time, 2 yr / 180 credits — competitive) and **Oxford (Saïd) AI Ethics, Regulation & Compliance Programme** (executive/online, Track B). For **Track A** depth the same CS-with-LLM spine as the copilots niche applies (Stanford **CS224N**; UC Berkeley **"LLM Agents" MOOC**, Dawn Song — includes an agent-safety module). No course code substitutes for a shipped red-team/eval portfolio.

**Acceptable postgraduate degrees `✓`** — **Track A:** MS or PhD in **CS / ML / AI** (or "related field, or equivalent experience" — the verbatim Apple Responsible-AI JD; **Anthropic's Alignment RE minimum is a "Bachelor's degree or equivalent"**) — a PhD is common at frontier labs but explicitly *not* required. **Track B:** MS in CS/Data Science, **or** a **JD/LLM or master's in law, technology policy, or AI-ethics/governance** (Cambridge, Oxford above). Postgraduate demand is thin — only ~6–7% of governance postings require a master's. **Distinctive `✓`: a portfolio outweighs a graduate degree for Track A** (this is applied engineering, not academic research) **and a certification does it for Track B.**

**Highly-valued certifications `✓`** — the niche's richest credential layer, and it holds the **domain moats**. *Track-B moats:* **IAPP AIGP** (the flagship AI-governance credential — no formal prerequisite, ~$799, exam 100 Q / 180 min; explicitly "governance-first, **not** for engineers building models"; BoK v2.1, Feb 2026 ⏱); **ISO/IEC 42001 Lead Auditor / Lead Implementer** (PECB, BSI, SGS; the "SOC 2 of AI", tiered by audit hours); **NIST AI RMF 1.0 Architect** (CIS). *Risk/audit/security bridge (A↔B):* **ISACA AAIA** (Advanced in AI Audit — requires CISA), **AAISM** (Advanced in AI Security Management — requires CISM/CISSP), **AAIR** (Advanced in AI Risk, launched 2026 `~` confirm prereq ⏱). *Track-A technical red-team/security:* **OffSec OSAI** (course AI-300; 24-hour practical), **EC-Council COASP**, **SANS/GIAC GAISP** (SEC545 / SEC411; OWASP-LLM + MITRE-ATLAS + NIST-RMF — GA rolling out through end-2026 ⏱). *Incumbents pivot in with* CISSP (ISC2), CISA/CISM/CRISC (ISACA), CIPP/CIPM (IAPP) — only ~12% of postings request any cert; the **AIGP + ISO-42001 + ISACA-AI family is the AI-specific layer stacked on top.**

**Experience & portfolio `✓`** — **Track A is more entry-accessible** (Apple's Responsible-AI role opens at **3+ yr**; Anthropic Alignment at **bachelor's + some ML/research-eng experience**). **Track B skews senior** (85% of postings target 5+ yr, only ~3% junior, ~12% senior at ~11 yr) — but **AIGP has no prerequisite, so a junior can hold it and stand out**. Portfolio currency by track: **A** trades on red-team reports, eval frameworks, published safety research, CTF / DEF CON AI-Village / HackAPrompt results, and `garak`/`PyRIT`/Inspect contributions; **B** trades on **certifications + shipped governance artifacts** — a completed AI-Act conformity assessment, an ISO-42001 AIMS, a model-risk policy, an audit report.

## 4. Skill Profile (JD-style)

**Required / must-have**
- Strong **Python**; software engineering (REST APIs, Git, Docker, a cloud, SQL); ML/DL literacy (**PyTorch / Hugging Face**)
- **LLM evaluation** — designing **human + automated evals** for non-deterministic systems (quality, safety, alignment); golden sets, **LLM-as-judge**, benchmark design; failure analysis & robustness testing
- **Red-teaming & adversarial testing** — jailbreaks, **prompt injection**; **OWASP LLM Top 10**, **MITRE ATLAS**; `garak` / **PyRIT** / **Promptfoo** / Inspect
- **Guardrails & runtime safety** — content/safety classifiers, **policy-as-code** (NeMo Guardrails, Llama Guard, OpenAI Guardrails); least-privilege tool use
- **Framework fluency (table stakes, both tracks)** — **NIST AI RMF**, **ISO/IEC 42001**, **EU AI Act**, GDPR — classify risk, map controls, produce conformity/model documentation (**model cards, system cards**)

**Preferred / differentiating**
- **Alignment & mitigation** — RLHF / preference methods, constitutional / classifier-based defenses, safety fine-tuning
- **Interpretability** — SAEs, **circuit tracing**, uncertainty & explainability (the auditable-evidence frontier); automated alignment auditing (**Petri**-style), scheming/deception & **CoT-monitorability** evals
- **AI-security depth** — model/data supply-chain, the **MCP / agent tool-calling attack surface**, CISSP→AAISM path
- **Governance-platform ops** — Credo AI, IBM watsonx.governance, Holistic AI, ServiceNow, OneTrust, Microsoft Purview
- **Domain (the moat):** mapping an **ML safety function** to its **EU-AI-Act high-risk classification** under the **EU Machinery Regulation (EU) 2023/1230** (applies 20 Jan 2027) and handing a clean package to the **Notified-Body** conformity process — bilingual with, but distinct from, the ISO-10218/13849 functional-safety engineer; you own the *model* side

**Tools & tech stack** — *Languages:* Python (+ SQL) · *Evals:* **UK AISI Inspect**, LLM-as-judge, **MLCommons AILuminate**, groundedness/faithfulness scoring · *Red-team:* `garak`, **Microsoft PyRIT**, **Promptfoo**, DeepTeam · *Guardrails:* **NVIDIA NeMo Guardrails**, **Llama Guard 4**, **OpenAI Guardrails**, Guardrails AI, Lakera · *Frameworks/standards:* EU AI Act, **NIST AI RMF (+ GenAI Profile 600-1)**, **ISO/IEC 42001**, OWASP LLM Top 10, MITRE ATLAS · *Governance platforms:* **Credo AI**, IBM watsonx.governance, Holistic AI, ServiceNow AI Control Tower, OneTrust, Microsoft Purview · *Models governed:* Claude, GPT, Gemini (+ open weights — e.g. Llama — for on-prem red-teaming)

**Bar by tier** — L1: runs evals + a red-team suite, or maintains a risk register mapped to a framework · L2: owns a model's eval/guardrail/red-team stack, or an AI-Act conformity workflow · L3: sets eval/red-team strategy, or runs the AIMS program (ISO-42001 Lead Auditor / ISACA territory) · L4/L5: owns model-release governance & safety-case direction, or EU-AI-Act conformity + regulator relationships.

## 5. Core Subject List `✓`
- **Foundational maths & CS:** Python + software engineering (APIs, testing, Git, Docker, cloud) · probability & statistics, linear algebra · ML/DL basics · **NLP, transformers, tokenization, embeddings** · the **AI lifecycle** (data → train → eval → deploy → monitor).
- **Core (governance & risk):** the three frameworks — **EU AI Act** (risk tiers, Art. 6 high-risk, GPAI Art. 53/55), **NIST AI RMF** (Govern/Map/Measure/Manage + GenAI Profile), **ISO/IEC 42001** AIMS · AI risk management; model/data documentation (**model cards, system cards**) · responsible-AI principles (fairness, transparency, accountability).
- **Niche-specific (assurance & security):** **safety evaluation** (human + automated evals, LLM-as-judge, benchmark design) · **red-teaming** (jailbreaks, prompt injection, adversarial testing) · **guardrails** (classifiers, policy-as-code) · **AI-system security** (**OWASP LLM Top 10**, **MITRE ATLAS**, model/data supply-chain) · conformity assessment & audit.
- **Frontier / advanced:** **alignment** (RLHF/preference methods, constitutional methods, anti-scheming training) · **interpretability** (SAEs, circuit tracing) · **CoT monitorability**, automated alignment auditing · **agentic-AI risk** (Excessive Agency, the MCP attack surface).
- **Domain (the differentiator):** the **EU Machinery Regulation 2023/1230 × AI-Act seam** — how an ML safety function becomes a high-risk AI system requiring Notified-Body assessment · reading standards & manuals to assemble a conformity package; and the boundary against **ISO 10218 / ISO 13849** robot functional safety (not yours to sign).

## 6. Training Programme Design & Duration `✓`
Software-conversion programme with a heavier **standards-reading** load than the copilots niche (three regulatory frameworks to internalize) but the same software spine — **no kinematics/hardware core**. ~1,200 hours; the dual-track shape means phase 2 leans Track-B (governance) and phases 3–4 lean Track-A (assurance engineering), converging on a shared capstone — which is why it runs slightly longer than the pure-copilot conversion.

| Phase | Focus | Fast-track | Thorough | ~Hrs | Outcome |
|-------|-------|-----------|----------|------|---------|
| 1. Foundations | Python/SWE, ML/NLP basics, transformers, the AI lifecycle | 1.5 mo | 5 mo | 260 | Ship a Python ML service |
| 2. Governance & risk frameworks | EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001, model/system cards | 1.5 mo | 4 mo | 220 | An AI-Act risk classification + control map (→ AIGP) |
| 3. Safety eval & red-teaming | Evals, LLM-as-judge, jailbreaks, OWASP-LLM/ATLAS, guardrails | 2.0 mo | 5 mo | 300 | An eval + red-team harness on a public model, written up |
| 4. **AI-security & alignment depth** | Supply-chain, MCP attack surface, RLHF/constitutional defenses, interpretability | 1.5 mo | 4 mo | 230 | A guardrail layer + a safety-case artifact |
| 5. **Domain specialization** + capstone | Machinery-Reg × AI-Act seam; ship a robotics conformity + assurance package | 1.5 mo | 3–4 mo | 190 | Job-ready evidence (both tracks) |
| **Total** | | **≈ 8–10 months** (full-time) | **≈ 18–24 months** (part-time) | ~1,200 | Junior AI-safety / AI-governance engineer |

*For an existing ML/software engineer, phases 3–4 (evals, red-teaming, security, alignment) are the fast path — ~3–5 months; for an incumbent GRC/privacy professional, phase 2 + the AIGP is the fast entry into Track B without the full engineering stack.*

## 7. Trending Research Topics `⏱ as of mid-2026`
> **Time-sensitive — re-research on a schedule.** `🆕` = 2025–26 release.
- **Regulation & standards (the compliance backbone — and it just moved):** **🆕 EU AI Act "Digital Omnibus"** — Annex III high-risk obligations postponed **2 Aug 2026 → 2 Dec 2027** (Art. 50 transparency + GPAI rules **NOT** delayed) · **🆕 GPAI Code of Practice** (10 Jul 2025; Transparency / Copyright / **Safety & Security** chapters; AI-Office fines switch on 2 Aug 2026) · **NIST AI RMF + 🆕 Generative-AI Profile (AI 600-1)** · **🆕 ISO/IEC 42001:2023** — the first *certifiable* AIMS, now "the SOC 2 of AI" (**SAP**, **Cornerstone** among certified vendors) · **🆕 US state patchwork** in force Jan 1 2026 (California **SB 53** frontier-transparency, Texas **TRAIGA**, Colorado repeal-and-replace).
- **Frontier-lab safety frameworks (what a governance engineer actually operates):** **🆕 Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy v3.0** (Feb 2026 — ASL thresholds + external-reviewed Risk Reports every 3–6 mo), OpenAI **Preparedness Framework**, **🆕 Google DeepMind Frontier Safety Framework v3.0** · **🆕 International AI Safety Report 2026** (Bengio, 30+ countries — the "IPCC for AI").
- **Alignment & safety science (making assurance falsifiable):** **🆕 Constitutional Classifiers** (Anthropic, Feb 2025 — jailbreak success 86% → 4.4% in eval, **[vendor-reported]**) · **🆕 deliberative alignment / anti-scheming training** (OpenAI × **Apollo Research**, Sep 2025) · **🆕 scheming / situational-awareness evals** (Apollo — now a standard safety-case line item) · **🆕 CoT Monitorability** (multi-lab position paper, arXiv 2507.11473) · **🆕 mechanistic interpretability** as practical discipline (Anthropic **circuit tracing**; a 2026 MIT Tech Review Breakthrough) · **🆕 Petri** automated alignment auditing (Anthropic, open-sourced Oct 2025; adopted by **UK AISI**).
- **Red-team, guardrail & eval tooling (converging on open cores):** **Microsoft PyRIT** (+ 🆕 AI Red Teaming Agent), **NVIDIA `garak`**, **🆕 Promptfoo** (acquired by OpenAI, Mar 2026 — stays MIT), **🆕 DeepTeam** (OWASP-LLM + Agentic mapped), **UK AISI Inspect** · guardrails: **NeMo Guardrails**, **🆕 Llama Guard 4** `~`, **🆕 OpenAI Guardrails** · benchmark: **🆕 MLCommons AILuminate v1.0** (12 hazard categories, 24k+ prompts). Method shift: **autonomous, agent-orchestrated red-teaming** embedded in CI/CD.
- **AI-security threat frameworks (the attack surface went agentic):** **🆕 OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications (2025)** — Prompt Injection still #1, new **System-Prompt Leakage** + **Vector/Embedding Weaknesses**, **Excessive Agency** the agentic anchor · **🆕 OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications** (Dec 2025) `~` · **🆕 MITRE ATLAS** (`~`v5.1, agent + **MCP-Server-Compromise** techniques added 2025–26). MCP / agent tool-calling is the fastest-growing attack surface in the niche.
- **Consolidation (proof the market is real):** AI-security M&A — **Palo Alto → Protect AI** (~$634M), **Check Point → Lakera**, **F5 → CalypsoAI**, **SentinelOne → Prompt Security**, **OpenAI → Promptfoo**; the standalone AI-security-tool era is folding into platform suites (open cores survive).

*Net trend: regulation is turning assurance into a compliance artifact (the EU AI Act mandates adversarial testing for high-risk + systemic-risk GPAI) — but with a real counter-signal: the EU delayed its own high-risk deadlines ~16 months, the US renamed its Safety Institute (**AISI → CAISI**, deliberately dropping "safety"), Gartner projects **>40% of agentic-AI projects cancelled by end-2027**, and the FLI AI Safety Index still grades every frontier lab inadequate. Treat jailbreak-reduction, adoption and "audit-ready in X days" figures as vendor-reported; the durable driver is enterprise risk/liability, not mandate.*

- **Niche-specific check (the moat):** given an ML-based safety function on a robot cell, can the candidate produce a correct **AI-Act high-risk classification under the Machinery Regulation 2023/1230**, cite the adversarial-testing obligation, and hand a clean package to a Notified-Body process — while correctly **not** claiming the ISO-10218/13849 functional-safety sign-off (that is the machine engineer's, not theirs)?
