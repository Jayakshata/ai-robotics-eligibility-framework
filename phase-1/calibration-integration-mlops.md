## 1. Definition & Scope
The **enabling "glue" layer** of applied robotics: making a robot's sensors, actuators and compute agree on geometry and time (**calibration**), assembling heterogeneous hardware + software into one working system (**integration & bring-up**), and building the data / training / evaluation pipelines that turn robot experience into deployable learned policies (**data infrastructure & MLOps**). It is the least glamorous but most load-bearing layer — every learned-robotics result rides on correct calibration, a working bring-up, and reproducible pipelines; the modern bottleneck in robot learning is increasingly *infrastructure, not algorithms*. This is a **two-headed niche**: a hardware-facing *integration/calibration* head (EE/embedded) and a software/data-facing *infrastructure/MLOps* head (distributed-systems/DevOps); strong engineers span both, but programs and credentials split cleanly. **In scope:** camera intrinsic + hand-eye (AX=XB) + robot-world (AX=YB) + camera-IMU spatiotemporal + kinematic/DH calibration & time-sync; requirements→spec→integration→test (V-model) bring-up, sensor/actuator interfacing, ROS 2 integration; robot data formats & pipelines (RLDS, LeRobotDataset, rosbag2/MCAP), dataset curation/versioning, experiment tracking, orchestration, model registry, serving, monitoring/drift, CI/CD-for-ML; large-scale distributed VLA/foundation-model training infra; continuous real-world/sim eval harnesses. **Out of scope (adjacent):** the perception/estimation algorithms that *consume* calibration (A1/A2 — this niche produces their calibrated, time-synced inputs); the learned policies/VLA architectures themselves (A7 — this niche trains, serves and evaluates them, but doesn't design them); generic middleware/real-time transport internals and safety/verification (A10 siblings). *Boundary note:* calibration machinery is shared with A1/A2, but this niche owns the **systems-glue and reliable-pipeline** problem — shipping and integrating, not deriving.

## 7. Trending Research Topics `⏱ as of mid-2026`
> **Time-sensitive — re-research on a schedule.** The field has consolidated into one open loop: *world-model/sim data-engines → embodiment-agnostic datasets → VLA policies → distributed real-world/neural-sim eval*, with calibration going markerless, differentiable and self-/continually-calibrating so the stack self-serves. These are the SAM2/DINO-equivalents the infra is built around. `🆕` = 2026 release; treat vendor benchmark claims as vendor-reported.

- **Foundation VLA policies (the models the infra serves):** **π0 / π0.5 + openpi** (Physical Intelligence flow-matching VLA; π0.5 [Sept 2025] adds open-world generalization via "knowledge insulation"; **openpi** is the de-facto open checkpoint/fine-tune repo — now with PyTorch + full-DROID training recipes; most-forked robotics VLA) · **NVIDIA GR00T N1** (2B, Mar 2025; dual-system slow-VLA 2–5 Hz / fast-policy 200 Hz+ humanoid FM) → 🆕 **GR00T N2** (GTC 2026, 30+ DoF humanoids) · **OpenVLA / Octo / SmolVLA** (the open baselines everyone fine-tunes; SmolVLA ships inside LeRobot).
- **Cross-embodiment data standards & formats (the center of gravity):** **Open X-Embodiment (RT-X)** — 2M+ trajectories, 22 robots, in RLDS/TFRecord; the canonical pretraining corpus · **LeRobot + 🆕 LeRobotDataset v3.0** (HF) — the open end-to-end stack (collect→train→eval→deploy) and emerging default format; v3 packs many episodes/Parquet+MP4-AV1 for **5–10× storage/loading** gains + Hub-scale streaming — the center of gravity for open robot MLOps · **DROID** — 76K in-the-wild trajectories, standard fine-tune/eval set · **cross-embodiment normalization** (active problem: delta-EE vs joint-space; latent-action / camera-frame / unified-TCP action reps — Latent Action Diffusion, SPACE).
- **Data-collection interfaces (scaling demos):** **UMI (Universal Manipulation Interface)** + variant explosion — DexUMI, FastUMI, MV-UMI, ActiveUMI, UMI-on-Legs, UMI-on-Air, 🆕 **YUBI** (2026, bimanual dexterity) — handheld/robot-free, embodiment-agnostic collection · **ALOHA / Mobile ALOHA** (reference bimanual teleop rig); 🆕 **BEAVR** (VR-teleop, accurate 6-DoF poses without SLAM wrist-tracking).
- **Synthetic data-engines & world models (the hottest 2026 thread):** **MimicGen / DexMimicGen** (ICRA 2025 — auto-expand a handful of demos into large valid datasets; DexMimicGen adds bimanual/dexterous/humanoid) → 🆕 **MoMaGen** (2026, multi-step mobile manipulation) · **NVIDIA Cosmos** (Cosmos-Transfer1 / Cosmos-Predict world models) + **GR00T-Dreams / Blueprint** — video world models generate demos, then latent-action/inverse-dynamics label them for VLA training; the 🆕 **GR00T Blueprint** pipeline (Teleop→MimicGen→Neural-Trajectory→Fine-tune) compressed **~6,500 collection-hours → 11 compute-hours / 780K trajectories** — "world-model-as-data-engine."
- **Simulation & training infrastructure:** **NVIDIA Isaac Lab** (open modular multi-embodiment RL/IL framework; foundation of the Isaac GR00T platform) · **Isaac Sim** (GPU-parallel, ~1000× real-time, thousands of instances; 🆕 new reference paper June 2026) · 🆕 **Isaac GR00T Reference Humanoid Robot** (2026 — first open Jetson-Thor humanoid reference design; Unitree + Stanford/ETH/AI2 committed — standardizes community hardware).
- **Evaluation & benchmarking infra (robot MLOps):** 🆕 **RoboArena** (2025, live through Dec 2026) — distributed, crowd-sourced, double-blind **real-world A/B eval** of generalist policies (4,284 episodes); the emerging "arena" standard vs unreproducible demos · 🆕 **RobotArena∞** (real-to-sim translation for scalable reproducible benchmarking) · 🆕 **RoboWorld** (2026) — neural-simulator eval replicating RoboArena in **~100 H100-hours** (slashes eval cost) · **SIMPLER**-style real-to-sim + 🆕 **ATOM-Bench** (atomic-skill/compositional generalization).
- **Calibration → markerless, differentiable, self/continual:** **EasyHeC / EasyHeC++** (markerless differentiable-rendering hand-eye, 2.06 mm → 1.35 mm) · **CtRNet / CtRNet-X, DREAM** (learned keypoint + PnP camera-pose) · 🆕 **Hydra** (2025, marker-free RGB-D hand-eye) · 🆕 **"Stable offline hand-eye calibration with one mark"** (Nov 2025 — any-robot single-mark robustness) · 🆕 **SurgCalib** (2026 — first **Gaussian-Splatting** differentiable hand-eye, surgical da Vinci) · 🆕 **Continual Hand-Eye Calibration for open-world manipulation** (2026 — online recalibration as robots run).
- **Systems integration & bring-up:** **ROS 2 + Isaac ROS / NITROS** (GPU-accelerated ROS 2 perception graphs; the bring-up story is now "train in Isaac Sim/Lab → deploy on Isaac ROS"; RosCon 2025 NVIDIA/OSRA contributions) · **Intrinsic Flowstate** (Isaac foundation models + Omniverse for industrial grasp/integration) · **LeRobot hardware-abstraction layer (SO-100/SO-101)** — unified driver interface abstracting arms/humanoids/teleop for reproducible bring-up.

*Net trend: infrastructure is collapsing into one self-serving loop — data-engines feed embodiment-agnostic datasets that train VLA policies, validated by distributed real-world/neural-sim arenas, while calibration and bring-up go markerless, differentiable and self-/continual. Several 2026 items are fresh preprints — treat vendor metrics as vendor-reported.*

## 12. Adjacent & Related Niches
The **enabling backbone** under most of the taxonomy. Produces the calibrated, time-synced inputs that **A1 perception** and **A2 state estimation / SLAM** consume; is the data supply-chain backbone for **A6 imitation learning & teleop/data pipelines**; trains, serves and evaluates **A7 VLAs / world models / synthetic data** (the infra the models run on); and closes the loop with **A8 simulation / digital twins** (sim-in-the-loop data engines & eval). Sits alongside its **A10 siblings** — robotics middleware (ROS 2/DDS transport, the layer below integration), embedded/edge AI & real-time systems (the on-robot deployment target), and safety/verification & runtime assurance. **Platform-agnostic** (every platform needs calibration, bring-up and a data/eval pipeline) — especially the **C1 fixed-arm** (hand-eye), **C2 mobile-manipulator** and **C5 humanoid** (fleet learning / data flywheel) tracks — and **cross-cutting across verticals** (B1 manufacturing bring-up & integration, B2 logistics fleet, B4 surgical hand-eye, B10 autonomous-vehicle calibration & data infra).
