## 1. Definition & Scope
The "platform" is **not a single body but a collective** — N robots (10s to 100,000s), homogeneous or heterogeneous (ground AMRs, nano-quadrotors, field bots), whose intelligence lives in the *interactions between* them. What makes it distinct as an engineering target: unlike every embodiment-centric platform (arm, humanoid, quadruped), the load-bearing problem is **not** the balance/grasp/locomotion of one machine but **coordination under decentralization, partial observability, and communication constraints**. The per-robot embodiment can be trivial — a $14 Kilobot — because the engineering *is* the algorithm plus the systems/comms substrate that lets many cheap bodies act as one. Scale, robustness, and emergence replace single-robot determinism: you design for graceful degradation as units drop out, not for a single deterministic controller. Two poles that hire differently: (a) a **swarm / distributed-algorithms research** pole (consensus, flocking, self-organization, MARL, MAPF), and (b) an **applied fleet-orchestration** pole (heterogeneous AMR/AGV fleets, traffic deconfliction, task allocation) — essentially distributed-systems software wearing a robotics hat.

## 2. Signature Capability Stack
- **A9 Multi-Robot Coordination / Swarm / Fleet Orchestration** *(load-bearing — the platform **is** this capability)* — consensus, formation/flocking, coverage (Voronoi), multi-robot task allocation (MRTA), fleet orchestration and traffic management. → `multi-robot-swarm`
- **A3 Planning & Navigation + MAPF** — per-agent Nav2 beneath the collective; **multi-agent path finding** (CBS / LaCAM) is the warehouse workhorse, sitting at the A3×A9 seam. → `motion-planning-navigation`
- **A2 State Estimation & Spatial AI** — collaborative / multi-robot SLAM, **relative localization**, GPS-denied positioning, shared/merged maps across the team. → `state-estimation-slam`
- **A10 Middleware / Comms / Real-time + Fleet Safety** *(systems substrate — unusually load-bearing here)* — DDS discovery at scale, mesh / UWB / 5G comms, VDA 5050 / Open-RMF interop, fleet-traffic deconfliction and deadlock avoidance. → `robotics-middleware-realtime`, `safety-verification-assurance`
- **A6 Robot Learning → MARL** — CTDE, QMIX / MAPPO, GNN-based decentralized policies, emergent coordination. → `reinforcement-learning-control`
- **A1 Perception** — distributed / **collaborative perception** (air-ground teams), multi-view fusion into a shared world estimate. → `computer-vision-3d-perception`, `multimodal-sensor-fusion`
- **A7 Foundation Models** *(emerging "fleet brain," frontier)* — DeepFleet / MAPF-GPT decision transformers, LLM-orchestrated heterogeneous teams. → `robot-foundation-models-vla`

## 3. Integration & Platform-Specific Skills
The "integration tax" no single capability owns: **decentralized-algorithm design** (provable consensus/convergence, scalability to 100s–1000s of agents, robustness to dropouts and Byzantine failures); **communication engineering under constraints** (mesh networking, UWB relative localization, bandwidth-limited and GPS-denied comms, and the notorious ROS 2 **DDS discovery bottleneck** at scale — the classic gotcha that kills naïve multi-robot bring-ups); **fleet interoperability** (VDA 5050 JSON/MQTT, Open-RMF fleet adapters, MassRobotics AMR Interop standard, lift/door/charger resource management across mixed vendors); **warehouse-scale MAPF** (lifelong MAPF, order-scheduling coupled to path planning); **sim-to-real for swarms** (GPU-vectorized MARL, then bridging to real radios and clock skew); and **collective safety** — deadlock/livelock avoidance and emergent-behavior assurance for a system with no single controller to certify. The distinctive bar: the **software / distributed-systems skill dominates the mechatronics skill** — atypical among platforms, and the reason this track hires unlike the others.

## 4. Typical Verticals
**B2 warehouse/logistics** — the largest and commercial anchor: dense AMR/AGV fleets are ~45% of the swarm market, with Amazon operating 1M+ robots orchestrated as one fleet. **B3 defense** — fastest-scaling and best-funded frontier: drone swarms, loitering munitions, collaborative autonomy. **B7 agriculture** — field-robot fleets for precision spray/weed and coverage farming. **B14 mapping/inspection & search-and-rescue** — collaborative coverage and air-ground teams that map or search an area in parallel. Also **marine / environmental monitoring** (underwater swarms). Chosen because each rewards **parallelism, coverage, redundancy, and graceful degradation** — jobs a single robot scales poorly at, where adding bodies buys throughput and fault tolerance rather than capability per unit.

## 5. Eligibility Profile
The archetypal **"swarm / multi-robot engineer"** bundles **coordination + planning/MAPF + state estimation + distributed-systems/middleware**, increasingly + **MARL**. Uniquely among platforms, **CS (distributed systems / algorithms) is the strongest single fit** — backend/distributed-systems experience partially transfers to fleet orchestration, which is rare in robotics. Also Tier-1: **Aerospace** (multi-vehicle GNC / formation flight — Caltech, Stanford, and MIT swarm groups sit in AeroAstro), **EE/ECE** (networked and decentralized control, consensus), **Applied Math** (graph theory, distributed optimization), and **Operations Research** (fleet = logistics optimization: assignment, VRP, scheduling). **ME/Mechatronics is the weakest differentiator here** — the value is coordination algorithms, not embodiment. Platform must-haves: **ROS 2 + multi-robot namespacing / DDS tuning + Nav2**, **Open-RMF / VDA 5050** fluency, and ≥1 swarm / MAPF / MARL project. Feeder capability profiles: `multi-robot-swarm`, `motion-planning-navigation`, `state-estimation-slam`, `reinforcement-learning-control`, `robotics-middleware-realtime`. Tiers: **L1** fleet integrator (Open-RMF / VDA 5050 deploy) → **L2–L3** coordination software (MAPF / nav / orchestration) → **L4/L5** swarm / MARL / multi-agent-FM research. The platform premium: a **real fleet coordinating robustly at scale** (single-robot demos are cheap; deconfliction and graceful degradation across a live mixed fleet are the differentiator).
