## 1. Definition & Scope
A **manipulation arm (or two) on a mobile base** — the `[◆]` integrative platform fusing the fixed-arm stack (C1) with an AMR (C3). The base is **wheeled** (differential, omni/Mecanum, or fully **holonomic** powered-caster) or **legged** (a quadruped/humanoid carrying an arm — a *loco-manipulator*). What makes it distinct as an engineering target: the robot must **navigate to where the work is** rather than have work fixtured to it, so the problem gains a mobility half — SLAM, navigation, a battery/compute budget — *on top of* the end-effector problem. The frontier tension is coordination: **grasping from a non-fixed base raises effective grasp error 3–5× vs. a bolted arm**, and multi-minute tasks accumulate SLAM drift, so the base pose is never the certainty a fixed cell gives. The payoff is amortizing one expensive arm across a workspace far larger than its reach.

## 2. Signature Capability Stack
- **A5 Manipulation & Grasping** *(load-bearing — still the point)* — pick/place, bimanual and loco-manipulation; the base only *positions* the arm. → `manipulation-and-grasping`
- **A3 Navigation + Motion Planning** *(the mobility half)* — autonomous navigation to a manipulation pose, then collision-free arm trajectories; base-placement (where to park for reachability). → `motion-planning-navigation`
- **A4 Whole-Body / Base–Arm Coordination** *(the integrative differentiator, `[◆]`)* — treating base + arm as one redundant kinematic chain; whole-body MPC, manipulability optimization, "navigate-then-manipulate" vs. unified control. → `whole-body-compliant-control`, `control-classical-optimal-mpc`
- **A2 SLAM & State Estimation** — grasp-pose estimation under base-localization uncertainty; drift management over long horizons. → `state-estimation-slam`
- **A1 Perception** — active/egocentric 3D perception from a moving viewpoint. → `computer-vision-3d-perception`
- **A6 Imitation / A7 VLAs** *(emerging "brain")* — end-to-end whole-body policies (Mobile ALOHA/ACT, RT-2, π0). → `imitation-learning-teleoperation`, `robot-foundation-models-vla`
- **A10 Middleware / Embedded** — untethered power, thermal and on-board GPU inference budgeting. → `robotics-middleware-realtime`

## 3. Integration & Platform-Specific Skills
The "integration tax" no single capability owns: **base–arm coordination** — choosing decoupled ("navigate to within reach, then move the arm"; ~5–15 s/repositioning), unified end-to-end, or hierarchical control, plus **whole-body MPC** over the combined DoF; **grasping from a non-fixed base** (localization-aware grasp planning against 3–5× error); **holonomic vs. nonholonomic base kinematics**; **the nav↔manipulation handoff** and SLAM-drift budgeting over long horizons; **mobile power/compute/thermal budgeting** for an untethered platform; a **dual safety envelope** — simultaneously an AMR (ISO 3691-4) *and* a moving cobot arm (ISO 10218); and **whole-body teleoperation rigs** (walk-behind, VR, phone), which matter because **mobile-manipulation datasets are 10–100× smaller** than fixed-base ones.

## 4. Typical Verticals
**B2 logistics / intralogistics** — the biggest commercial pull: case handling, truck unload, palletizing, and mobile pick-and-place (Boston Dynamics Stretch; NEURA X MOVE 1200 + MAiRA M). **B9 service / healthcare** — hospital supply, med and lab-sample delivery (Diligent Moxi). **Lab automation / science** — self-driving labs and the "mobile robotic chemist" (Liverpool). **B1 flexible manufacturing** — one arm roving between machines/cells, cleanroom (KUKA KMR iiwa / KMR iisy CR). **B9 domestic / elder care** — household chores and daily-living assistance (Stretch trials, TidyBot++). Chosen because each spans a **workspace larger than one arm's reach in human/brownfield spaces too big or costly to fixture**.

## 5. Eligibility Profile
The archetypal **"mobile-manipulation engineer"** is bilingual: it fuses the **C1 arm bundle** (manipulation + motion planning + force control + calibration + perception) with the **AMR stack** (SLAM + navigation + state estimation), joined by the integrative **whole-body / base–arm coordination** skill — the hard part is *stitching the two subsystems*. Strongest backgrounds: **Robotics / Mechatronics / EE / ME** for the embodiment (whole-body kinematics, base drive, power/compute); **CS / AI** for perception and learning. Platform must-haves: **ROS 2 + Nav2 + MoveIt 2** wired together, base–arm coordination, SLAM, and mobile-manipulation teleoperation/data literacy. Feeder capability profiles: `manipulation-and-grasping`, `motion-planning-navigation`, `state-estimation-slam`, `whole-body-compliant-control`, `calibration-integration-mlops`, `imitation-learning-teleoperation`. Tiers: **L1** integrator (marry an AMR to an arm) → **L2–L3** mobile-manip software (nav + manip + coordination) → **L4/L5** learned whole-body mobile-manipulation research. Platform premium: a proven **autonomous grasp from a moving base** and a whole-body-coordinated deployment.

## 7. Trending & Notable `⏱ as of mid-2026`
**End-to-end learned mobile manipulation** — **Mobile ALOHA** (ACT policy, ~50 demos, co-training +up to 90%) and **TidyBot++** (holonomic + phone-teleop data) are the reference open platforms; the **data bottleneck** (10–100× smaller datasets) drives teleop-rig work. **VLAs generalizing to mobile bases** — **Google Gemini Robotics** (ALOHA-2 fleet, 12 mo data), **RT-2** (Everyday Robots), **Physical Intelligence π0/π0.5**, **GR00T N1**. **Legged loco-manipulation RL** — RoboDuet, GeCCo (generalist contact-conditioned policy), dynamic pick-and-place on quadruped+arm. **Commercial scaling** — Stretch to **DHL (1,000+ units)**, truck unload/palletizing; **Diligent Moxi 2.0** (NVIDIA AI, GTC Oct 2025; ~100 robots, **1.25M deliveries**, 25+ hospitals; **acquired by Serve Robotics Jan 2026**); **NEURA** end-to-end intralogistics (LogiMAT 2026); SAP physical-AI pilots. *(Re-research before publish.)*
