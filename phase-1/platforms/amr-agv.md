## 1. Definition & Scope
A **wheeled ground robot that moves payloads or sensors autonomously across a 2D floor plane**. The engineering target splits on *how it localizes*: an **AGV** follows fixed infrastructure (magnetic tape, inductive wire, laser reflectors, QR grid) on a pre-set path and simply stops for obstacles; an **AMR** uses **natural-feature navigation / SLAM** to localize and re-plan on existing floors with **zero infrastructure**. The boundary is blurring — modern vehicles share SLAM stacks — but the AMR is the AI-relevant form factor. What makes it distinct as an engineering target: with the base wheeled and the workspace **unbounded and shared with people and other robots**, the whole problem collapses onto **navigation** — *where am I, where is everything else, how do I get there safely* — with no arm, grasp, or balance to solve. Sub-forms: goods-to-person picking robots, cart/tote movers, tuggers, unit-load/pallet AMRs, autonomous forklifts, and outdoor sidewalk/last-mile bots.

## 2. Signature Capability Stack
- **A2 State Estimation & SLAM** *(load-bearing — the AMR's core problem)* — infrastructure-free localization, LiDAR/visual SLAM, AMCL against a prior map, loop closure, re-localization across floors/shifts. → `state-estimation-slam`
- **A3 Planning & Navigation** — global + local path planning, dynamic obstacle avoidance, costmaps, recovery behaviors; **Nav2** is the reference stack. → `motion-planning-navigation`
- **A1 Perception + Sensor Fusion** — 2D/3D LiDAR + stereo/fisheye camera + IMU + wheel-odometry fusion; free-space, obstacle, and people/forklift detection. → `multimodal-sensor-fusion`, `computer-vision-3d-perception`
- **A9 Fleet Orchestration / Multi-Agent** *(the AMR differentiator)* — traffic management, deadlock/conflict resolution, task allocation across dozens–thousands of robots, VDA5050 / Open-RMF interoperability. → `multi-robot-swarm`
- **A10 Safety** — safety-rated laser scanners, speed-and-separation, ISO 3691-4 protective stops. → `safety-verification-assurance`
- **A10 Middleware & Integration** — ROS 2 real-time nav stack; WMS/WES/MES and elevator/door integration. → `robotics-middleware-realtime`, `calibration-integration-mlops`
- **A8 Simulation / A7 VLAs** *(emerging "brain")* — warehouse digital twins for fleet validation; natural-language task commands and fleet-optimization foundation models. → `simulation-digital-twins`, `robot-foundation-models-vla`

## 3. Integration & Platform-Specific Skills
The "integration tax" no single capability owns: **fleet traffic management** (lane deconfliction, mutex zones, intersection/deadlock handling); **multi-vendor interoperability** via **VDA5050** (master-control ↔ vehicle messaging) and **Open-RMF** (shared lifts/doors/space); **AMR functional safety** — **ISO 3691-4:2020** and **ANSI/A3 R15.08**, safety-rated scanner field-set design, speed-and-separation zones, **UL 3100** battery safety; **sensor + odometry calibration and map maintenance** in changing environments; **WMS/WES/MES integration** and order-to-motion choreography; **drive-base kinematics** (differential, omni/mecanum) plus **battery/charging/uptime** engineering; and **commissioning / site-survey** work (mapping a live facility). The distinctive bar: keeping a fleet **productive and safe amid people, moving pallets, and other vendors' robots**.

## 4. Typical Verticals
**B2 logistics & warehouse** (goods-to-person picking, tote/cart transport, pallet moves, autonomous trailer un/loading, sortation) — the AMR's native, fastest-growing home (~33% of the market). **B14 inspection & maintenance** (indoor/outdoor patrol, meter reading). **B9 service/domestic** & **B4 healthcare** (hospital supply/pharmacy delivery, hospitality, autonomous floor scrubbing). **B15 food** and **B1 manufacturing intralogistics** (line-side feeding, tugger trains). Plus outdoor **last-mile sidewalk delivery**. All need **repeatable point-to-point transport through a shared, semi-structured, human-occupied space** — exactly what a wheeled base with SLAM optimizes.

## 5. Eligibility Profile
The archetypal **"AMR / mobile-robot navigation engineer"** bundles **SLAM/localization + planning & navigation + perception/sensor fusion + fleet orchestration + safety**, increasingly + **robot-learning**. Strongest backgrounds: **CS / Robotics / EE** for the perception-planning-SLAM stack; **ME / Mechatronics** for the drive base, safety, and integration. Platform must-haves: **ROS 2 + Nav2**, **SLAM** (Cartographer / slam_toolbox / LiDAR-inertial), sensor calibration, a **fleet / VDA5050 or Open-RMF** exposure, and **AMR-safety** (ISO 3691-4 / R15.08) literacy. Feeder capability profiles: `state-estimation-slam`, `motion-planning-navigation`, `multimodal-sensor-fusion`, `multi-robot-swarm`, `safety-verification-assurance`. Tiers: **L1** deployment/commissioning (site survey, mapping, WMS hookup) → **L2–L3** navigation software (SLAM + Nav2 + perception) → **L4** fleet-orchestration / traffic → **L5** learned-navigation / social-nav research. Platform premium: a **real fleet deployed safely in a live facility**.

## 6. Platform-Specific Hardware & Stack `~`
**Reference platform:** **NVIDIA Isaac AMR** with **Nova Orin / Nova Carter** dev kit (Segway **RMP Lite 220** drive base + **Jetson AGX Orin** + 4 stereo + 4 fisheye + 2×2D + 1×3D LiDAR, running **Isaac Perceptor**). **Commercial AMRs:** MiR (**MiR100–MiR1350**), OTTO Motors (heavy unit-load), Locus **Origin** (goods-to-person), Geek+ (P-series / RoboShuttle), Fetch/**Zebra**, Seegrid (vision-guided), Exotec **Skypod**, Amazon **Proteus** (400 kg cart), Fox Robotics **FoxBot Mk3** (autonomous forklift), Avidbots **Neo** (scrubber), Diligent **Moxi** (hospital). **Sensors:** SICK **microScan3** / Hokuyo safety scanners, **Ouster / Velodyne** 3D LiDAR, Intel **RealSense**, IMUs. **Compute:** NVIDIA **Jetson Orin / Thor** edge modules. **Software:** **ROS 2 + Nav2 + slam_toolbox/Cartographer + AMCL**; **NVIDIA Isaac ROS** (Perceptor, cuVSLAM, nvblox) + **Isaac Sim / Gazebo** digital twins; fleet layer — **VDA5050 + Open-RMF**, NVIDIA **Isaac Mission Dispatch**, InOrbit/OTTO **VDA5050 Connector**, Amazon **DeepFleet**.

## 7. Trending & Notable `⏱ as of mid-2026`
**Fleet interoperability standardization** — **VDA5050 V3.0** (VDMA) as a scalable toolkit; **Seegrid** targeting full compliance in 2026; **Open-RMF** hitting multi-fleet milestones (lifts/doors, mutex groups). **Fleet-scale AI** — Amazon deployed its **millionth robot** and a fleet-wide **DeepFleet** foundation model (~**10%** travel-efficiency gain); **next-gen Proteus** (June 2026) takes **natural-language** task commands and roams full sites. **Physical-AI / foundation-model navigation** and **VLM-based social/crowd-aware navigation** are the research frontier (Arena-Rosnav 4.0, HuNavSim, SocNav benchmarks). **Software revenue now out-pacing hardware** (~18% CAGR); **RaaS/subscription** models spreading (e.g. Moxi). Consolidation: **Serve Robotics acquiring Diligent Robotics** (Jan 2026). Caveat: high-throughput **deterministic fixed-path AGVs persist** where cost/uptime dominate. *(Re-verify before publish.)*
