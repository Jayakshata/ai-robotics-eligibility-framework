## 1. Definition & Scope
The **assurance layer that makes an autonomous robot trustworthy enough to deploy** вҖ” *proving* it will stay safe, *monitoring* that it actually does at runtime, and *detecting & recovering* when something fails. It fuses four intertwined disciplines: **functional safety** (standards-governed hazard analysis, SIL/ASIL, the safety lifecycle), **formal methods** (model checking, theorem proving, reachability, neural-network verification вҖ” establishing properties *before* deployment), **runtime assurance & monitoring** (Simplex/RTA, runtime verification, safety filters/CBFs, shielding вҖ” *enforcing* safety online), and **fault detection, isolation & recovery / FDIR** (analytical redundancy, observer-based diagnosis, safe fallback). Its distinctive positioning: it is the **least "robotics-department" niche in Axis A** вҖ” its center of gravity is CS-theory / formal-methods groups and *dedicated safety-critical-systems engineering*, not a generic Robotics degree вҖ” and it is the **only Axis-A niche with a mature, industry-mandated professional-certification market** (TГңV / exida functional-safety credentials) that is frequently a **hard hiring gate**, and the only one where **standards literacy** (IEC/ISO/UL/DO) counts as much as coursework. **In scope:** functional safety & standards; formal verification (model checking, theorem proving, reachability, NN verification); runtime assurance/monitoring (RTA/Simplex, runtime verification, safety filters/CBFs, shielding); FDIR; safety & assurance cases (GSN/AMLAS). **Out of scope (adjacent):** the *synthesis* of safe controllers themselves (A4 вҖ” CBFs/safe-MPC are used here as filters but the controller design is owned there), the execution/behavior architectures they hook into (A3 вҖ” behavior trees & autonomy stacks), and the middleware / hard-real-time A10 siblings (the substrate assurance *runs on*, owned separately).

## 2. Role Archetypes & Seniority Tiers
| Tier | Title | Mandate |
|------|-------|---------|
| **L1** | Junior Safety / Verification Engineer | Runs hazard analyses (FMEA/FTA) and writes/executes verification cases under supervision; implements runtime monitors and safety checks; encodes specs (STL/LTL); assembles evidence for the safety case. |
| **L2** | Functional Safety / V&V Engineer | Owns a safety subsystem end-to-end (hazard analysis вҶ’ safety requirements вҶ’ verification вҶ’ runtime monitor); applies a standard (ISO 26262 / IEC 61508 / DO-178C) to a real product; builds and validates a safety filter / RTA wrapper on real hardware. |
| **L3** | Senior Safety Assurance / V&V Engineer | Designs the **assurance architecture** вҖ” decides which properties get formally *verified* vs. *runtime-monitored* vs. *tested*; owns the safety case; makes standard-compliance and tool-qualification calls; sets the verification strategy; mentors. |
| **L4/L5** | Staff / Principal Safety Architect / Research Engineer | Sets the assurance strategy/research agenda; advances formal-verification / runtime-assurance / assurance-of-ML SOTA; represents the org in standards committees; publishes; org-wide safety authority вҖ” often the **safety-case owner / sign-off** with legal-of-record weight. |

## 3. Eligibility Criteria вҖ” Acceptable & Highly-Valued Credentials

**Acceptable undergraduate degrees** вҖ” Tier 1 (auto): CS (esp. with AI/ML), Robotics Engineering, EE/ECE, AI. Tier 2 (with evidence): Mechanical Engineering, Mechatronics, Maths, Physics. **A10-specific reweighting `вң“` (a large re-tilt toward theory + standards вҖ” the inverse of the embodied-skills niches):** **CS with a theory / formal-methods emphasis** (logic, automata, programming languages, verification) is the clearest Tier-1 top вҖ” the *rare* niche where **pure-theory CS outranks hands-on robotics CS**; **Electrical / Systems / Aerospace Engineering** with **reliability, fault-tolerance, GNC and DO-178C/ARP4754** exposure rises to Tier 1 (aerospace is the historical home of RTA, Simplex and safety cases); **Software Engineering** (V&V, requirements, safety lifecycle) and **Applied Maths / Logic** (model checking, reachability) rise. **Robotics / Mechatronics HOLDS вҖ” qualifying only with transcript proof of formal methods, embedded/real-time or safety-standards coursework** (most robotics majors carry none of the logic/standards spine). A **pure ML / DL-heavy CV or RL degree** *without* logic, verification or safety exposure ranks **relatively lower** here than in perception/learning niches вҖ” necessary for the learning-enabled *component*, but it under-weights the assurance core.

**Highly-valued undergraduate programs `вң“`** (CS-theory + embedded/real-time + reliability вҖ” *not* the robotics brand; UG depth is thinner than the postgrad market by design) вҖ” **CMU `15-414 Bug Catching: Automated Program Verification`** (Platzer track; model checking, SAT/SMT, symbolic execution вҖ” the strongest single UG marker) В· **UPenn `CIS 4710 / ESE 5410`-family embedded & real-time systems** В· **Oxford / Imperial** CS logic-&-verification cores В· EE / Aerospace **reliability-engineering** courses.

**Acceptable postgraduate degrees** вҖ” MS/PhD in a **dedicated Safety-Critical / Dependable / System-Safety Engineering** program (the single strongest match вҖ” *outranks* a generic Robotics MSc), CS with a formal-methods/verification specialization, EE/Systems/Aerospace (reliability, GNC, DO-178C), Software Engineering (V&V), or Applied Maths/Logic; Robotics/CS/AI only with a demonstrated formal-methods-and-standards spine.

## 4. Skill Profile (JD-style)

**Required / must-have**
- Strong **discrete math, mathematical logic & automata** (the verification substrate вҖ” load-bearing here in a way it is in no other Axis-A niche)
- **Hazard & risk analysis**: HAZOP, **FMEA, FTA, STPA**; risk assessment; **SIL / ASIL** determination
- **Functional-safety standards & lifecycle**: IEC 61508, ISO 26262, ISO 13849, ISO 10218-1/-2 + ISO/TS 15066, ISO 21448 (SOTIF), DO-178C
- **Formal methods**: temporal logic (**LTL / CTL / STL / MTL**), model checking (**SPIN, NuSMV**), **SAT/SMT** solving
- **Runtime verification & monitoring**: monitor synthesis, the **Simplex / RTA architecture** (ASTM F3269 вҖ” Input Manager / Safety Monitor / RTA Switch / Recovery)
- **Safety filters**: **control barrier functions (CBFs)**, **Hamilton-Jacobi reachability**, shielding
- **Reachability analysis** of hybrid / closed-loop systems (zonotopes; CORA / Flow*)
- **FDIR**: fault-tolerant control, analytical redundancy, observer-based diagnosis, reliability engineering
- **Safety & assurance cases**: **GSN**, assurance arguments
- **Embedded / hard-real-time** systems fundamentals; **Python + C++**; ROS 2 integration on real hardware

**Preferred / differentiating**
- **Neural-network verification** (**Оұ,ОІ-CROWN, Marabou, NNV, ERAN/DeepPoly**; the **VNN-COMP** benchmark)
- **Closed-loop / learning-enabled verification** (**Verisig, POLAR, JuliaReach, NNV** for NN-controlled hybrid systems)
- **Safe RL** вҖ” shielding (+ verification-guided shielding), learned CBFs, **constrained RL / CMDP**
- **Assurance of ML / foundation-model & VLA safety** (**AMLAS**; SafeVLA-style CMDP alignment; layered guardrails)
- **Conformal-prediction runtime monitors**; **OOD / failure detection** & safe fallback
- **Verified AI / scenario-based assurance** (**VerifAI + Scenic**); **probabilistic model checking** (PRISM, Storm)
- **Theorem proving** (Coq, KeYmaera X); **neural Lyapunov / barrier-certificate** synthesis
- **Tool qualification** (DO-330) and certification-authority liaison
- Publications at **CAV / TACAS / HSCC / EMSOFT / RV / ICCPS / SAFECOMP**, or standards-committee participation

**Tools & tech stack** вҖ” *Languages:* Python, C++, some Julia В· *Model checking / theorem proving:* **KeYmaera X**, SPIN, **NuSMV / nuXmv `~`**, UPPAAL `~`, Z3 / CVC5 `~`, Coq В· *Reachability:* **CORA**, Flow*, JuliaReach, ReachNN В· *NN & closed-loop verifiers:* **Оұ,ОІ-CROWN**, Marabou, NNV, ERAN/DeepPoly, Verisig, POLAR В· *Probabilistic MC:* **PRISM**, Storm В· *Spec / runtime monitoring:* **STLCG++**, RTAMT `~`, MoonLight `~` В· *Scenario / verified-AI:* **Scenic + VerifAI** В· *Safe control / safe RL:* HJ reachability (DeepReach / helperOC `~`), CBF toolboxes, **SPARK** (humanoid), **Safety-Gymnasium + SafePO / OmniSafe `~`** В· *Safety-engineering:* FTA/FMEA/STPA & GSN tooling (Ansys **medini analyze** `~`, Astah GSN `~`) В· *Sim / middleware:* MuJoCo, Isaac Sim/Lab, ROS 2 В· *Standards library (as load-bearing as any dataset):* IEC 61508 В· ISO 26262 + ISO/PAS 8800 В· ISO 21448 SOTIF В· UL 4600 В· DO-178C/DO-330 В· ISO 10218-1/-2 + TS 15066 В· ASTM F3269

**Bar by tier** вҖ” L1: solid on hazard analysis + a model checker + a runtime monitor in sim В· L2: applies a standard to a real product and ships an RTA/CBF safety filter on hardware В· L3: architects the assurance stack (verified-vs-monitored-vs-tested split) and owns a safety case В· L4/L5: advances SOTA / sits on a standards committee / holds sign-off authority.

*Net trend: assurance is consolidating on a **layered stack** вҖ” Оұ,ОІ-CROWN-class verified certificates at the bottom, learned CBF/HJ-reachability *filters* in the middle, conformal + FM-fallback runtime monitors on top вҖ” wrapped around VLA/foundation-model policies and stress-tested on VLA & humanoid safety benchmarks. Many 2026 items are fresh preprints; re-verify at publish.*

## 11. Assessment & Validation
- **Knowledge checks** per subject-list theme: write LTL/STL specs; hand-derive a small model-checking or SAT/SMT result; an FMEA/FTA + SIL/ASIL determination; a CBF forward-invariance argument.
- **Project rubrics** with objective metrics: **verified-property coverage** (fraction of safety requirements formally *proven* vs. only tested) В· **constraint-violation rate** (safe RL / RTA) В· **runtime-monitor detection latency + false-positive/negative rate** В· **reach-avoid set volume / conservativeness** В· **NN-verification success + solve time** (VNN-COMP-style) В· **fault-detection & recovery success** В· **safe-fallback trigger correctness**.

- **Recognized certifications as evidence (uniquely weighty in A10):** a **TГңV / exida functional-safety personnel cert** (CFSP / CFSE) or documented **ISO 26262 / IEC 61508 / DO-178C** project sign-off вҖ” frequently the *hard gate*; plus a formal-methods MOOC (Platzer LFCPS / Seshia 219C / Vechev RTAI).
- **Senior tiers:** portfolio (a verification tool / RTA system / a written safety case) + a **safety-case review / assurance-argument defense** (defend a GSN/AMLAS argument) + system-design interview (*"given this learned policy, design the assurance stack and justify what is verified vs. monitored vs. tested"*) + live counterexample / failure debugging.
