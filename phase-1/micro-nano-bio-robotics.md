## 1. Definition & Scope
Building robots at the **extremes of scale and of biological inspiration** — machines too small to carry a battery, or whose design and actuation are borrowed from (or built out of) living systems. **In scope, five poles:** (1) **micro/nano robotics** — sub-millimetre agents *fabricated* by MEMS / soft-lithography / two-photon printing and driven by external fields (magnetic, electrostatic, optical, acoustic) or catalysis, for targeted drug delivery, cell/molecule manipulation, and minimally-invasive medicine; (2) **bio-inspired robotics** — extracting locomotion, actuation, and morphology principles from animals (flapping-wing flight, legged millirobots, gecko adhesion, CPGs, morphological computation); (3) **bio-hybrid / living robots** — machines actuated by muscle cells, bacteria, or whole insects (biobots, cyborg insects); (4) **aerial manipulation** — coupled UAV-plus-arm dynamics and contact interaction *in flight*; and (5) **hardware-software co-design** — jointly optimizing **morphology↔control** and **compute↔power** (embedded / TinyML) for resource-constrained embodiments — with **haptics / scaled tele-robotics** as the human-in-the-loop dual for micromanipulation. **The defining trait vs the rest of Axis A: physics and fabrication dominate, not learning.** At small scale the physics *inverts* — surface forces beat body forces, flows are low-Reynolds, and there is no room for onboard power or computation — so the robot is *fabricated*, not assembled from catalog parts, and *actuated externally* or by living cells. This is the **least CS-centric niche in the taxonomy**; its intelligence often lives in the *body* (morphological computation) as much as in a policy. **Out of scope (adjacent homes):** general soft-robotics modeling/control (A10 soft robotics), the tactile transducer stack and haptic-device psychophysics (A1 tactile/force — the haptics home), generic legged/aerial locomotion control (A5 locomotion), and the swarm-coordination theory microswarms borrow (A9 multi-robot).

## 2. Role Archetypes & Seniority Tiers
| Tier | Title | Mandate |
|------|-------|---------|
| **L1** | Junior Micro/Bio-Robotics Engineer | Fabricates & handles micro/bio devices under supervision (cleanroom / soft-litho / casting, or cell culture); operates field-actuation & flight-test rigs; collects data; implements micro-vision / tracking pipelines. |
| **L2** | Micro/Bio-Robotics Engineer | Owns a device subsystem end-to-end (fabrication→actuation→closed-loop control, *or* one bio-hybrid actuator, *or* an aerial-manip payload); characterizes it; demonstrates a closed-loop task on real hardware. |
| **L3** | Senior Micro/Bio-Robotics Engineer | Designs the device + actuation architecture; makes actuation-modality & fabrication-process tradeoffs; sets scaling-law / physics targets and benchmarks; owns morphology↔control co-design calls; mentors. |
| **L4/L5** | Staff / Principal Micro/Bio-Robotics Research Scientist | Sets the research agenda; advances medical-microrobot / bio-hybrid / insect-scale-flight / co-design SOTA; publishes in *Science / Nature / Science Robotics / Nature Machine Intelligence*; org-wide direction. |

*Three poles run through every tier: the **fabricator/physicist** (MEMS, materials, scaling-law physics, or bio/wet-lab), the **control/autonomy** engineer (micro-visual-servoing, magnetic / RL swarm control, aerial-manip planning), and the **co-design** engineer (morphology↔control + compute↔power). Strong seniors bridge fabrication and control — the field's scarce, high-value profile. Note the credentialing venue skews to **Science / Nature-family journals**, not the RSS/CoRL/ICRA circuit of the learning-heavy niches.*

## 3. Eligibility Criteria — Acceptable & Highly-Valued Credentials

**Acceptable undergraduate degrees** — Baseline (grounding-ref): Tier 1 (auto) CS-with-AI/ML, Robotics, EE/ECE, AI; Tier 2 (with evidence) ME, Mechatronics, Maths, Physics. **This niche *inverts* the usual CS-forward weighting `✓` — the robot is governed by fabrication + scaling-law physics + external-field actuation, not learned policies:**
- **Mechanical Engineering rises to Tier 1** — microfabrication / MEMS, fluid mechanics, mechanism & dynamics of fabricated micro-structures; the dominant degree across the ETH / EPFL / Toronto / MIT / Harvard micro & bio labs.
- **EE / Applied Physics rises to Tier 1** — electromagnetics & electrostatics (field-based actuation), MEMS, micro-sensor read-out, and the embedded / co-design half.
- **Newly first-class (niche-unique, rise from "rarely listed"):** **Materials Science & Engineering** (soft / magnetic / stimuli-responsive materials + micro/nano-fabrication), **Applied Physics / Nanotechnology / MEMS** (scaling-law physics, low-Re dynamics, micro-optics), **Bioengineering / BME / Cell-&-Molecular Biology / Chemistry** (bio-hybrid actuators, cell manipulation, drug delivery, biocompatibility — a genuine first-class path, unique to this niche + tactile), **Aerospace / Aeronautical** (for the aerial-manipulation sub-scope — flight dynamics, GNC).
- **Pure CS/AI / data-science falls — insufficient alone.** CS stays useful for closed-loop micro-vision, swarm algorithms, and emerging RL navigation, and is *central* to the co-design / TinyML sub-scope — but a CS-only candidate without physics / fabrication / materials / bio literacy is weak here; perception/NLP-flavored ML profiles carry little weight.
- **Niche gate (replaces the generic "≥1 robotics course / ROS capstone"):** ≥1 course in **microfabrication/MEMS *or* scaling-law micro-physics *or* bio-hybrid/biomechanics**, plus a fabricated-hardware or micro/bio artifact.

**Highly-valued undergraduate programs `✓`** — micro/nano is overwhelmingly graduate/research, so UG is a **feeder**; weight a fabrication + dynamics core *plus* an actual micro/bio course: **Harvard SEAS** (**ES 159/259 *Introduction to Robotics***, Wood — dedicated MEMS/microrobotics/soft/biomimetic lectures; on-ramp to the Harvard Microrobotics Lab / Wyss), **MIT Course 2 / MechE** (**2.740 *Bio-inspired Robotics***, Sangbae Kim), **EPFL Microengineering (SMT) BSc** (the standout small-scale feeder → ME-436), **ETH / TUM (MAVT / ME+EE)** dynamics + microfab foundation (their microrobotics is MSc-level), **UC Berkeley / Stanford / Michigan / WPI** (general robotics + a bio-inspired / dynamics course as feeders into Fearing / Cutkosky / bio labs).

**Acceptable postgraduate degrees** — MS/PhD in Robotics, ME, EE / Applied Physics, **Materials Science**, or **Bioengineering** with a micro/nano, bio-inspired / bio-hybrid, aerial-manipulation, or co-design focus. (Center of gravity is ME / EE / Materials / BioE — not CS.)

## 4. Skill Profile (JD-style)

**Required / must-have**
- **Scaling-law physics & micro/nano multiphysics** — surface forces ≫ body forces, adhesion, capillarity, Brownian motion; **low-Reynolds-number hydrodynamics / microfluidics**
- **Microfabrication / MEMS literacy** — soft lithography, PC-MEMS "pop-up", two-photon 3D printing, cleanroom basics (or the bio equivalent: cell culture / tissue engineering)
- **Electromagnetics & field-based actuation** — magnetic (gradient / rotating fields), electrostatic, piezoelectric, dielectrophoretic, optical / acoustic, catalytic / chemical
- **Dynamics & control of underactuated / externally-actuated systems** — kinematics/dynamics, feedback control, state estimation **re-cast for non-inertial, field-driven agents**
- **Micro-visual-servoing** — closed-loop control & localization under a microscope (or motion-capture for insect-scale flight)
- Strong **Python + C/C++**; **embedded / real-time** firmware for actuation drivers & flight controllers
- **ROS 2** for macro test rigs / aerial platforms
- Experiment design & data collection on **fabricated hardware** (budgeting hardware / wet-lab debug friction)

**Preferred / differentiating**
- **Bio-hybrid actuation** — muscle-cell / cardiac / bacteria-powered actuators; tissue engineering; closed-loop bio-actuation; cyborg-insect interfacing
- **Magnetic microswarm control** and **robotic cell / molecule / nanomaterial manipulation**
- **Learned control for microrobots** — RL / imitation for magnetic navigation & swarm control (the 2025–26 wave)
- **Biomedical microrobot translation** — biodegradable / biocompatible materials, in-vivo US / MRI / X-ray tracking & steering
- **Bio-inspired locomotion** — flapping-wing flight, legged millirobots, gecko adhesion, CPGs, morphological computation
- **Aerial manipulation** — coupled UAV+arm dynamics, whole-body kinodynamic planning, aerial grasping; language-conditioned aerial manip (VLAs)
- **Morphology↔control co-design** (evolutionary / differentiable / generative) and **compute↔power co-design** (TinyML: pruning, quantization, NAS, on-device training)
- **Scaled bilateral micro-teleoperation & haptics** (the human-in-the-loop dual)
- Publications in **Science / Nature / Science Robotics / Nature Machine Intelligence / IJRR**; **MARSS / IEEE-NANO / RoboSoft**

**Tools & tech stack** — *Languages:* Python, C/C++ (+ embedded C / MCU firmware) · *Middleware:* ROS 2 · *Actuation & navigation:* electromagnetic coil systems (Helmholtz / Maxwell pairs, **OctoMag / MiniMag**-class 8-coil rigs, **MagnebotiX MFG-100** `~`), function generators / power amplifiers, motion capture (**OptiTrack / Vicon**) · *Fabrication:* **Nanoscribe** two-photon microprinting `~`, soft-lithography / PDMS + cleanroom, laser micromachining, **PC-MEMS pop-up**; (bio) mammalian cell culture, 3D bioprinting, microfluidics · *Micro-vision:* high-speed / inverted microscopy, OpenCV tracking · *Sim:* **COMSOL** multiphysics FEM + CFD (low-Re, EM fields), MuJoCo / Isaac Sim/Lab / PyBullet (bio-inspired dynamics), **co-design suites — SoftZoo, Evolution Gym, PlasticineLab, SoftGym, FluidLab** · *Learning:* PyTorch, RL libs, LeRobot (teleop) · *Co-design / embedded:* **TinyML stack** (TensorFlow Lite Micro, efficientml.ai tooling), MCU / TinyML dev boards · *Aerial:* **PX4 / ArduPilot** flight stacks, mocap flight volume

**Bar by tier** — L1: solid on the required list; fabricates & runs a device in a rig · L2: ships a device subsystem (a characterized micro-device, a bio-hybrid actuator, or an aerial-manip payload) with closed-loop control · L3: architects device + actuation systems, deep in ≥1 differentiating area (medical micro, bio-hybrid, aerial-manip, or co-design) · L4/L5: advances SOTA — a clinical microrobot, a novel bio-actuator, insect-scale autonomy, or a co-design method — publishes / sets direction.

| Advanced | **Learned control for microrobots** — RL (model-based or Turbo-style transformer-RL) for magnetic navigation/swarm, sim-to-real | RL + domain randomization for micro-actuation; sim-trained policy transfers to a real rig |
| Advanced | **Bio-hybrid actuator** — culture muscle cells on a scaffold (or a cyborg-insect interface) and drive closed-loop contraction | Bio-actuation + wet-lab; measurable, controllable contraction / steering |
| Advanced | **Aerial manipulation** — UAV+arm (or cable-suspended) aerial grasp with whole-body planning; optional language-conditioned (VLA) | Coupled flight + contact dynamics; a successful in-air grasp/place |
| Advanced | **Morphology↔control co-design** in SoftZoo / Evolution Gym; deploy a co-designed body in sim | Co-optimization; co-designed morphology beats a hand-designed baseline on the task metric |
| Capstone | A **fabricated closed-loop micro/bio system** end-to-end — e.g., an autonomously-navigated magnetic microrobot in a vascular phantom, an insect-scale flyer, or a bio-hybrid swimmer — with metrics | End-to-end frontier stack; a real fabricated device performs an autonomous task with a reported success rate |

## 12. Adjacent & Related Niches
This niche is a **bundle spanning several homes**. It shares fabrication with **A10 soft robotics** (soft actuators, compliant mechanisms — the closest sibling). **Haptics / tele-robotics** is the human-facing dual, homed in **A1 tactile/force sensing** (scaled bilateral micro-teleoperation). **Aerial manipulation** pairs with **A5 locomotion** (aerial), **A3 motion planning**, and **A4 control**, with its dynamics/perception backbone in the locomotion niche (UZH / UPenn / LAAS). **Learned micro/swarm control** draws on **A6 RL/imitation** and **A9 multi-robot / swarm**; **aerial-manip & co-design VLAs** touch **A7 VLAs / world models**. **HW-SW co-design** spans **A8 differentiable simulation** (morphology↔control) and **A10 embedded / edge AI** (compute↔power / TinyML). **Biomedical microrobots** feed the **B4 healthcare/surgical** vertical (FDA / IEC overlay); bio-inspired flyers & cyborg-insects feed **B11 aerial/UAS** and defense / search-&-rescue; microrobots also feed **lab automation**. Platform tracks: primary **C10 micro/nano**, plus **C8 soft/continuum**, **C6 aerial/UAV**, **C9 swarm**, and it feeds **C4 quadruped / C5 humanoid** bio-inspired locomotion.
