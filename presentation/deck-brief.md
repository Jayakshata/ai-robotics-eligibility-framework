## 3. The strategic decision (DECIDED — execute, don't relitigate)

Five depths of engagement with the government skilling system. Present as a **ladder**; the recommendation is fixed.

| Model | Government mechanism | Effort/Capex | BGR Neo's control & brand |
|---|---|---|---|
| A. Training Partner (deliver govt courses, paid per trainee) | TNSDC / NSDC empanelment | Low, fast | Low — one vendor among many |
| B. Naan Mudhalvan knowledge partner (courses inside colleges, train-the-trainer) | State curriculum track | Low–medium | Medium — big reach, shallow depth |
| **C. State CoE — OUR RECOMMENDATION** (own flagship AI-robotics centre) | **TNSDC CoE (EOI selection)** | Medium–high | **High — marquee, industry-anchored** |
| D. National CoE / Future Skills Centre | MSDE / NSDC | High | High but Delhi-centric, competitive |
| E. Dedicated SPV (own skilling company w/ govt) | PPP / Section 8 SPV | Highest | Highest — full ownership |

**RECOMMENDED:** **Model C — a state-first CoE via TNSDC, structured so it can spin into an SPV (Model E) later.** Because (1) it is the only model that fully uses BGR Neo's real-robot advantage; (2) it is winnable via a mechanism that already exists; (3) it leaves the bigger national/SPV plays open. Models A and B become *feeders* (colleges spot talent → CoE trains the best deeply).

**The wedge / how we stand out — the spine of the whole pitch:** Anyone can teach AI on a laptop. Almost nobody can put a fresh grad in front of a **real industrial robot and a real factory integration project** — but that is literally BGR Neo's day job as a system integrator. Every other CoE applicant offers classroom AI; BGR Neo offers the actual shop floor. This wins the EOI *and* makes the training better than anyone else's.

## 4. Government ecosystem facts (USE THESE — do not invent schemes)

Real and current as of mid-2026. Ground the deck in them so it reads as credible, not aspirational.

- **National CoEs for Skilling (MSDE):** central government has launched National Centres of Excellence for Skilling specifically targeting **AI, robotics, advanced manufacturing**, delivering **NSQF-aligned** training (levels ~5–8), operationalised via National Skill Training Institutes.
- **TNSDC / TNSKILL** (Tamil Nadu Skill Development Corporation): the **state nodal agency**. Runs a **Centre of Excellence programme** where industry partners set up high-end skilling facilities, selected via an **Expression of Interest (EOI)** decided by a committee under the **TNSDC Managing Director**. Partner eligibility = qualified technical experts, a detailed investment plan, and clean audited financials (3 yrs). *Primary mechanism for Model C.*
- **Naan Mudhalvan** (TN CM's flagship): embeds industry skill courses into engineering-college curricula as mandatory credit programmes via a **Training-of-Trainers** model; targets 10+ lakh students. *This is Model B — the "reach/feeder" reference.*
- **NSQF + Qualification Packs (QPs):** national certification runs through **Sector Skill Councils** that notify competency standards under the National Skills Qualification Framework. Certificates are nationally recognised when courses map to QP/NOS.
- **Two relevant Sector Skill Councils** (because scope = "all types of AI"):
  - **IASC SSC** (Instrumentation, Automation, Surveillance & Communication) — covers **industrial robotics** (operating & programming manufacturing robots), digital manufacturing, IIoT, sensors. *BGR Neo's home turf → specialization tier.*
  - **NASSCOM FutureSkills / IT-ITeS SSC** — NSDC-approved QPs for **19 AI & big-data job roles**. *The software-AI side → foundation tier.*
- **Precedents to name in the deck:**
  - **TNASDCL** (Tamil Nadu Apex Skill Development Centre for Logistics) — a Section 8 not-for-profit **PPP/SPV** formed by TNSDC + a sector skill council + industry (TVS). *The exact template for Model E later.*
  - **TN Semiconductor Mission CoEs** — set up as **for-profit special-purpose vehicles** with academia/industry. *Proof a CoE can be structured to earn, not just cost.*

> ⚠ **Verify before filing.** These are safe for an internal deck. Before an EOI actually goes to TNSDC, re-confirm the EOI eligibility criteria and both SSC/QP claims at source.

## 6. The eligibility framework (simplify from the Eligibility Matrix)

Two-tier, **assessment-gated**, adjusted for *government + fresh grads*:

- **Gate = a short entrance assessment**, not a hard degree wall. A government programme is judged on employability and inclusion, so a "direct-fit degrees only" filter works against the scheme's own goals.
- **Degree bands (soft):** direct-fit (CS, ECE/EEE, Mechatronics, Robotics/Automation, Instrumentation, Mechanical) → adjacent-with-bridging (Physics, Maths, other engineering + programming) → needs-major-bridging.
- **Prerequisites:** basic programming + maths foundation; freshers show projects/internships.
- **Map entry to QP/NSQF levels** so the certificate is nationally recognised.

Present as a **clean one-slide matrix**, not a wall of text. Leadership asked for "the base" — this is it.

## 7. The training programme (from Common Core vs Specialization + Progression Paths + Lab Procurement)

**Two tiers:**

1. **Common foundation (everyone), ~920 h:** maths, Python, basic ML, robotics fundamentals, industrial/safety context — delivered **simulation-first** (cheap: GPU workstations + RoboDK/Gazebo/Isaac Sim). Maps to entry-level QPs (NASSCOM AI QPs + IASC basics).
2. **Industrial specialization (the differentiator), ~480 h:** deep hands-on on **real robot cells** — vision, manipulation, welding OLP, digital twin, integration — on **real BGR Neo shop-floor problems**. Maps to higher NSQF levels (5–8) via IASC SSC QPs.

**Lab story (cost-aware):** foundation = mostly software/sim (low capex); real hardware only appears at the specialization stage. Tier the equipment list from the **Lab Procurement & Partnerships** synthesis into Foundation vs Specialization.

> **No fabricated prices.** `phase-2/lab-procurement-and-partnerships.md` deliberately carries no rupee/dollar figures — only relative cost signals (Entry `LOW` / Mid `MEDIUM` / Flagship `HIGH`). The deck therefore presents the **structure** of the capex envelope and leaves the numbers to finance. Its strongest cost line comes straight from that source: ~80% of capability niches are exercisable at Entry/Mid, and frontier spend buys humanoids and research — not factory skills. **BGR Neo is mostly buying computers, not robots, because the robots are already ours.**
