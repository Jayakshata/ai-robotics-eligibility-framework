# Phase 2 — Cross-Niche Eligibility Matrix

**Purpose.** A single cross-niche view of *who is eligible for what*: the strongest degrees, named flagship postgraduate programs, key certifications, and entry-experience bar for each of the 23 Axis-A capability niches — plus the inverse ("which degree opens which niches") and the notable per-niche eligibility shifts. This is the credential/eligibility layer of the AI-for-robotics program, synthesized from every Phase-1 profile's §2 (Role Archetypes) and §3 (Eligibility).

**Grounding.** Baseline degree tiers, canonical certifications and the two-tier eligibility rule come from `phase-1/_grounding-reference.md` §A/§C. Every per-niche cell is drawn from that niche's Phase-1 profile §3 (the "highly-valued / strongest" rankings) and §2 (the entry-tier mandate). Confidence tags follow the source: `✓` research-grounded · `~` to-verify at publish.

**How to read the matrix.** Per the grounding ref, **CS (with AI/ML), EE/ECE, Robotics Engineering and AI are baseline Tier-1 (auto-qualifying) for every niche**; ME, Mechatronics, Maths and Physics are baseline Tier-2 (qualify with robotics/AI evidence). The "Strongest undergrad" column therefore shows only the **niche-specific signal** — which baseline degree is *the strongest*, which Tier-2 or off-list degree **↑ rises** to Tier-1, and which **↓ falls** to non-differentiating. A cell is niche-specific, not a repeat of the baseline.

## Part 2 — Reverse View: Which Degree Opens Which Niches

*"Tier-1 (strongest)" below means the niche's §3 explicitly names the degree as the strongest/dominant fit or **↑ raises it to Tier-1**, not merely baseline auto-qualification. Baseline note repeated because it matters: CS, EE/ECE, Robotics Eng and AI auto-qualify everywhere — the lists show where each is genuinely **differentiating**.*

**Computer Science (esp. with AI/ML)** — baseline Tier-1 for all 23; *single strongest / dominant* for:
- Task & Motion Planning (with AI) · Imitation Learning · Robot Foundation Models & VLA (with DL/NLP) · Sim-to-Real · World Models · Multi-Robot/Swarm (distributed-systems track) · Calibration/Integration/MLOps (infra head) · Safety/Verification (theory/formal-methods track) · Simulation (with graphics) · Middleware (systems track).
- *Ranks conditional / lower* (needs a controls, dynamics or systems transcript): Control · Whole-Body Control · Locomotion · Middleware (pure-ML CS does **not** auto-qualify) · Soft Robotics · Micro/Nano/Bio.

**EE/ECE (incl. Computer Engineering)** — baseline Tier-1; *strongest / rises to top* for:
- Computer Vision & 3D Perception (**strongest perception major**) · Multimodal Sensor Fusion · State Estimation/SLAM (**most "EE-flavored" niche**) · Tactile & Force Sensing (the sensor-side degree) · Classical/Optimal Control (co-top) · Whole-Body Control (EE-with-controls) · RL for Control (EE-with-controls) · Robotics Middleware (**Computer Engineering = the headline degree**) · Calibration/Integration (bring-up head) · Micro/Nano/Bio (EE/Applied Physics).

**Mechanical Engineering** — baseline Tier-2; **↑ rises to Tier-1** for the embodied/physical niches:
- Manipulation & Grasping · Whole-Body/Compliant Control (**the default degree**) · Locomotion · Classical Control (dynamics modeling) · Simulation & Digital Twins · Soft Robotics (**the default**) · Micro/Nano/Bio (**dominant degree**) · Tactile & Force Sensing (force/contact half).
- *Explicitly falls / non-differentiating* in: CV, Sensor Fusion, SLAM, Motion Planning, **TAMP (sharpest fall)**, RL, Sim-to-Real, VLA, World Models, Multi-Robot, Middleware, Safety, Calibration/MLOps.

**Robotics Engineering** — baseline Tier-1 across all 23 as the integrative degree; *strongest* where systems-integration & embodiment dominate:
- Calibration/Systems Integration (**WPI "Unified Robotics" = the archetype**) · Manipulation · Locomotion · Whole-Body Control (embodiment backbone).
- *Explicitly outranked by a specialized degree*: Control (dedicated **Systems & Control** MSc wins) · Middleware (dedicated **Embedded Systems** MSc wins; Robotics HOLDS only with a systems spine) · Safety/Verification (dedicated **Safety-Critical Systems** MSc wins; a control-first Robotics MSc is *not* best-fit).

**AI / ML** — baseline Tier-1; *strongest / co-top* for:
- Task & Motion Planning (a dedicated AI degree = clearest T1 top) · Robot Foundation Models & VLA (co-Tier-1 with CS; **a pure ML/AI master's is uniquely competitive**) · Imitation Learning · RL for Control · Sim-to-Real · World Models.

**Maths / Applied Maths** — baseline Tier-2; **↑ rises to Tier-1 / near-T1** for the math-first niches:
- Motion Planning · Classical Control · RL for Control · Multi-Robot/Swarm (graph theory — effectively T1) · Sim-to-Real (near-T1) · World Models · Computer Vision (projective geometry/SVD) · Sensor Fusion & SLAM (estimation theory) · TAMP (logic/discrete) · Simulation (numerical methods) · Safety/Verification (Applied Maths/Logic — model checking).

**Physics** — baseline Tier-2; **↑ rises** for the modeling-heavy niches:
- Sim-to-Real (near-T1) · World Models (**↑T1** — stat-mech/stochastic processes) · Simulation (Computational Physics) · Computer Vision (optics/image formation) · Micro/Nano/Bio (Applied Physics **↑T1**) · Tactile (Applied Physics/MEMS).

**Biomedical / Bioengineering (BME)** — off the general robotics list; **first-class only** for:
- Micro/Nano & Bio-Inspired Robotics (bio-hybrid actuators, cell manipulation, drug delivery — a genuine first-class path) · Soft Robotics (biohybrid, exosuits, surgical continuum) · Tactile & Force Sensing (haptics psychophysics, neuromorphic e-skin, via BME/Neuroscience). *Plus the Axis-B Healthcare & Surgical vertical.*

**Aerospace / Aeronautical** — off-list / Tier-2 generally; **↑ rises to Tier-1** across the estimation-&-autonomy spine (Kalman filtering, inertial nav, GNC, trajectory optimization are aerospace-native; MIT 16.485, Stanford AA 273/274/228 literally live in Aero/Astro):
- State Estimation/SLAM · Multimodal Sensor Fusion · Motion Planning · Classical Control · Multi-Robot/Swarm · TAMP · Whole-Body Control (T1-adjacent) · Locomotion (aerial sub-niche) · Simulation · Safety/Verification (DO-178C/ARP4754) · Micro/Nano/Bio (aerial-manipulation).

**Mechatronics** — baseline Tier-2, electromechanical-integration flavored; *strongest as the integration/embodiment/hardware feeder* rather than a sole Tier-1:
- **↑ rises** for Calibration/Systems Integration (mech+elec+software+sensing) · Imitation Learning (the **teleop-rig / data-infra lane**) · HRI/Cobots (pHRI hardware, contact safety) · embodiment feeders (Manipulation, Locomotion, Tactile sensor design, Whole-Body).

*Specialist degrees that appear nowhere on the general list but are genuine first-class feeders for specific niches: **Photogrammetry / Geomatics / Geodesy / Surveying** (CV, SLAM, Sensor Fusion) · **Materials Science** (Soft, Tactile, Micro/Nano/Bio) · **HCI / Cognitive Science / Psychology** (HRI only) · **OR / Industrial Engineering** (TAMP, Multi-Robot, RL) · **Ocean / Naval / Marine Engineering** (underwater Locomotion only) · dedicated **Systems & Control**, **Embedded Systems** and **Safety-Critical Systems** MScs (Control, Middleware, Safety — each outranks a generic Robotics MSc).*

## Part 3 — Notable Per-Niche Eligibility Shifts

The baseline "big three + AI" (CS/EE/Robotics/AI Tier-1; ME/Mechatronics/Maths/Physics Tier-2) holds as a floor, but the per-niche re-weightings are where eligibility actually gets decided. The interesting deltas:

**1. Mechanical Engineering's split personality — the single biggest cross-niche swing.** ME **rises to Tier-1** for every *embodied/physical* niche — Manipulation, Whole-Body & Compliant Control, Locomotion, Soft Robotics, Micro/Nano/Bio, Simulation — where it is often *the default degree*. It **falls to non-differentiating** for every *intelligence* niche — perception, SLAM, planning, RL, sim-to-real, VLA, world models. The cleanest illustration is the **Manipulation ↔ TAMP mirror**: ME is the strongest rise for Manipulation and the sharpest fall for its decision-making twin, Task & Motion Planning.

**2. EE/ECE owns perception + estimation; Computer Engineering owns the systems layer.** EE/ECE is the strongest single major for **Computer Vision** (the strongest of *any* perception niche), **Multimodal Sensor Fusion**, and **State Estimation/SLAM** (the "most EE-flavored" niche) — signals/DSP, detection-&-estimation theory and sensor electronics are the spine. One rung down the A10 stack, **Computer Engineering** becomes the *headline* degree for Middleware/Real-Time (embedded, computer architecture, OS) — an explicit reversal away from a generic Robotics degree, and the one place a pure-ML CS degree does **not** auto-qualify.

**3. The ML-master's-beats-robotics-degree cluster (A6–A7).** For **Robot Foundation Models/VLA, World Models, Sim-to-Real and Imitation Learning**, a **pure ML/AI master's — not even titled "robotics" — is uniquely competitive**: transformer/tokenization/RLHF and deep-RL/representation experience transfers directly. Correspondingly, **Maths/Statistics and Physics rise to near-Tier-1** here (sharpest in World Models & Sim-to-Real, where the job is probabilistic/generative modeling, not embodiment), while ME/actuation backgrounds carry the least weight of anywhere in Axis A. ETH Zürich's `263-5911 "Robot Learning: From Fundamentals to Foundation Models"` is the only flagship whose *title is the niche*.

**4. Aerospace's quiet ubiquity across the autonomy spine.** Off the general list, Aerospace/Aeronautical **rises to Tier-1** for **SLAM, Sensor Fusion, Motion Planning, Control, Multi-Robot/Swarm and TAMP** — because Kalman filtering, inertial navigation, GNC and trajectory optimization are aerospace-born, and the defining courses literally sit in Aero/Astro departments (MIT 16.485 VNAV; Stanford AA 273 / AA 274 / AA 228). It also carries the *aerial* sub-niche of Locomotion and the aerial-manipulation slice of Micro/Nano/Bio.

**5. Off-list specialist degrees that unlock exactly one cluster.**
- **Photogrammetry / Geomatics / Geodesy / Surveying** → **3D perception, SLAM, Sensor Fusion** (bundle adjustment and multi-view geometry *originate* in photogrammetry; Bonn's lab is literally "Photogrammetry & Robotics"). A riser almost no other A-niche shares.
- **Materials Science & Engineering** → **Soft Robotics** (the *signature* lift — elevated further than in any other niche), **Tactile Sensing**, **Micro/Nano/Bio** — because here the *materials are the actuators and sensors*.
- **Biomedical / Bioengineering** → **Micro/Nano/Bio** (bio-hybrid) and **Soft Robotics**, plus the tactile/haptics psychophysics slice — and it is the natural degree for the Axis-B **surgical** vertical.
- **HCI / Cognitive Science / Psychology / Design** → **HRI only** — the single niche where human-science degrees become first-class.
- **OR / Industrial Engineering** → the *decision/logistics* niches: **TAMP, Multi-Robot/Fleet, RL** (MDPs, scheduling, vehicle routing, approximate dynamic programming).
- **Ocean / Naval / Marine Engineering** → the **underwater** slice of Locomotion only.

**6. Three niches where a dedicated non-robotics MSc outranks a "Robotics MSc."** In the systems/theory-tilted corners of A4/A10, the generic Robotics master's is *not* the best-fit degree: **Control** → dedicated **Systems & Control** (TU Delft DCSC, KTH); **Middleware/Real-Time** → dedicated **Embedded Systems** (TU Delft CESE, KTH); **Safety/Verification** → dedicated **Safety-Critical Systems** (University of York + AAIP/AMLAS). Each profile states the inversion explicitly.

**7. Certification texture is wildly uneven — and often the honest answer is "there isn't one."** Most niches are **portfolio/research-credentialed** with thin formal certs: Tactile, Soft, Micro/Nano/Bio, VLA, World Models and TAMP all state outright that recognition comes from published work + a portfolio, not a badge. Two exceptions stand out: **Safety/Verification is the only Axis-A niche with real, valued, experience-gated *professional* certifications** (exida/TÜV **CFSE/CFSP**, **ISO 26262**), and **Simulation/Digital Twins is the only niche with a purpose-built formal exam** (**NVIDIA NCP-OpenUSD**). Conversely, the general gold-standard MOOC **Northwestern *Modern Robotics* is explicitly *weak/irrelevant*** for SLAM, Middleware and Calibration/MLOps — a useful backbone, not niche evidence. The niche-native cutting-edge credential is **Hugging Face LeRobot** (Imitation Learning, VLA, Manipulation), which still carries **no formal certificate** `~`.

**8. The rare "self-taught / non-degree" on-ramp.** Only two niches explicitly welcome non-degree entrants as *first-class*: **Robotics Middleware** (deep embedded-Linux/RTOS/C/concurrency experience) and **Calibration/Integration/MLOps** (a DevOps/SRE or data-platform engineer converts directly to the infra head). Everywhere else the theory bar keeps a degree load-bearing.
